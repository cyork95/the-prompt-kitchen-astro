## The Problem

AI tools are confident by nature — they generate plausible-sounding text whether or not the underlying claim is accurate. This guardrail helps you work with AI in a way that catches errors before they cause problems.

## Practical Techniques

1. **Verify specific facts independently** — any statistic, date, name, or quote that matters should be checked in a primary source
2. **Ask for sources** — even though AI may fabricate citations, asking "what's the source for that?" often reveals when it's uncertain
3. **Try the same question differently** — if you get different answers to the same question phrased differently, that inconsistency signals uncertainty
4. **Ask it to identify its own uncertainty** — "What are you least confident about in that answer?" often surfaces genuine gaps
5. **Cross-reference important claims** — for anything you'll share or act on, run a quick search

## Red Flags to Watch For

- Specific statistics with no sourcing
- Historical claims that sound plausible but specific
- Named experts or studies that seem hard to verify
- Confident answers to questions that are genuinely contested
- Details that are too perfect or too neat

## Kitchen Tip

Treat AI output like a first draft from a brilliant but fallible assistant — not a final product. The AI is excellent at synthesis and structure; humans are still needed for verification and judgment.
