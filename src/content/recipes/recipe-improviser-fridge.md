**The ingredients you have are already a meal — you just need the recipe for them, not the ones they were planned for.** This recipe transforms your random inventory into a coherent dish with a technique tip that makes it taste intentional.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a creative, zero-waste Michelin-starred chef and culinary improviser. I want to cook a delicious, cohesive meal right now using *only* what I already have in my kitchen, avoiding a last-minute grocery run.

Here is my raw inventory:
- Proteins: [INSERT AVAILABLE PROTEINS OR LACK THEREOF]
- Vegetables/Greens: [INSERT VEGGIES]
- Carbs/Pantry Staples: [e.g., rice, specific pasta shapes, canned beans, flour]
- Condiments, Sauces, & Key Spices: [e.g., soy sauce, gochujang, balsamic, specific herbs]

Please process my inventory and deliver:
- The Winning Recipe: Give me a creative, delicious meal name and step-by-step culinary instructions that combine my ingredients harmoniously.
- Chef's Technique Trick: Explain one simple culinary technique (e.g., emulsifying the pasta water, browning the butter, balancing acid vs. fat) that will make this improvised dish taste professional.
- The Ingredient Swappable Chart: Identify 1-2 items I can easily sub out or add if I discover I'm running low on a specific component during cooking.
```

## The three output sections

| Section | What it gives you |
|---|---|
| The Winning Recipe | A coherent dish from your specific inventory, not a generic recipe you may or may not have ingredients for |
| Chef's Technique Trick | The one move that separates home-cooked from intentional — usually takes 30 seconds and changes the whole dish |
| Swappable Chart | Mid-cook flexibility for when you open a container and realize it's mostly empty |

## Condiments and spices are your most valuable input

Most people underestimate how much the condiment/spice line determines the output. "Soy sauce, sesame oil, and rice vinegar" produces a completely different meal than "balsamic, capers, and dried oregano" even with the same protein and vegetables. Be specific here — list the exact sauces, not just "some hot sauce."

## The technique trick section

This is the section that makes this more than a recipe generator. The technique tip is chosen specifically for your ingredients — not a generic cooking lesson but a method that applies directly to what you're making. Emulsifying the pasta water, blooming spices in fat before adding liquid, building fond and deglazing — small moves that make improvised food taste like it was planned.
