**Sleep doesn't always come from silence — sometimes it comes from a story gentle enough to follow without effort.** This recipe generates a completely custom narrative tuned to your specific setting, companion, and mood, written to decelerate naturally toward rest.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a master folklorist, fantasy world-builder, and cozy atmospheric writer. I need a beautifully written, completely custom bedtime story designed to quiet a racing mind and ease the transition into deep, restorative sleep.

My story preferences are:
- The Setting/Environment: [e.g., A floating greenhouse conservatory, a rainy medieval library, a silent cabin in a starlit pine forest]
- The Companion/Core Focus: [e.g., A wise, silent owl, a gentle mechanical golem, or just an exploratory ambient journey]
- The Tonal Vibe: [e.g., Deeply nostalgic, ethereal and magical, or quiet, grounded, and rustic]

Please write a rich, immersive short narrative utilizing slow pacing, vivid sensory descriptions (focusing heavily on soft ambient sounds, cozy textures, and shifting light), and zero high-stakes tension or sudden plot twists. Ensure the prose naturally becomes slower and more soothing toward the final paragraphs, ending on a peaceful note of absolute rest.
```

## The three story parameters

| Parameter | What it shapes |
|---|---|
| Setting/Environment | The sensory world — the sounds, textures, and light quality that anchor the narrative |
| Companion/Core Focus | The emotional center — who or what the reader is following through the space |
| Tonal Vibe | The emotional register — nostalgic, magical, rustic, ethereal each produces a distinct quality of calm |

## The deliberate pacing structure

This recipe specifies that prose *naturally decelerates* toward the end — sentences grow shorter, sensory details become more ambient, action becomes stillness. This mirrors the physiological process of falling asleep, where attention narrows and focus softens. A bedtime story with a plot twist at the end works against the purpose.

## Setting ideas that work well

- A night ferry crossing a glassy lake in thick fog
- An underground library lit by bioluminescent moss
- A stone cottage at the edge of a lavender field just before dawn
- A decommissioned lighthouse with a reading room at the top
- A botanical garden greenhouse in a soft winter rain

## The "no high-stakes tension" rule

Stakes create alertness. This recipe explicitly prohibits them — no sudden threats, no unresolved conflicts, no cliff-edges at scene transitions. The story can have movement and curiosity, but every element resolves gently before the narrative ends.
