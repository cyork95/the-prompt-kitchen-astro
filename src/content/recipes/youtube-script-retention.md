Most YouTube drop-off happens in the first 60 seconds — and again at the formal outro. This recipe builds a script structure that front-loads the value signal, maintains tension through the middle, and exits without the "if you liked this video" outro that tells viewers the video is over.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an elite YouTube scriptwriter and audience retention expert. I need a script outline for a video that maximizes Watch Time and Average Percentage Viewed (APV) by eliminating fluff and maintaining constant narrative tension.

My video topic is: [INSERT TOPIC]
My target audience is: [INSERT TARGET AUDIENCE]

Structure the script using a high-retention framework divided into these precise beats:
- The Hook (0-15s): Validate the title/thumbnail promise, create an open loop, and micro-dose the payoff without giving away the ending.
- The Re-Hook (15-60s): Establish the stakes — why solving this problem or hearing this story matters right now.
- The Core Content Delivery: Break down the main points using "Pacing Spikes" (alternating between deep information and pattern interrupts/visual cues).
- The Transition to Click: Instead of a formal outro that signals the video is ending, seamlessly bridge the final thought into a recommendation for my next video.

Provide the structured outline with specific scripting cues, tonal directions, and retention notes.
```

## The four beats explained

| Beat | Seconds | Goal | Common mistake |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hook | 0–15s | Validate the thumbnail promise + open a loop | Restating the title verbatim, slow intros |
| Re-Hook | 15–60s | Raise the stakes — why this matters now | Jumping straight into content without stakes |
| Core Content | Variable | Deliver value with pacing spikes | Long unbroken information blocks cause drop-off |
| Transition to Click | Final 30s | Bridge to next video without signaling "end" | "Don't forget to subscribe" — this kills retention |

## The open loop technique

The hook opens a question the viewer needs answered to leave. The worst hooks state everything upfront. The best hooks reveal just enough to make leaving feel like losing:

*"Most people think X — but after doing Y, I discovered something that completely changed how I think about this."*

The viewer needs to know what you discovered. They stay.

## Pacing spikes

Every 90–120 seconds, interrupt the information flow with a pattern break: a cut to B-roll, a quick story beat, a visual, a one-sentence cliffhanger before the next point. This resets viewer attention without breaking the content flow.
