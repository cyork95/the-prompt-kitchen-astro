**Meal prep fails because it's boring.** Identical containers of chicken and rice last two days before you hate them. This recipe builds a modular system — a small set of batch-prepped components you recombine into different meals — so variety doesn't require extra cooking time.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a clinical nutritionist and time-efficiency meal prep specialist. I need a sustainable meal planning system that ensures I eat clean, whole-food meals every single day without spending hours cooking or throwing away wasted groceries at the end of the week.

My dietary boundaries and constraints are:
- Dietary Focus/Preferences: [e.g., High-protein, macro-conscious, Mediterranean, vegetarian]
- Time Budget: [e.g., Under 20 minutes of active cooking per meal, or a single 2-hour prep block on Sundays]
- Health/Caloric Goals: [e.g., Maintaining muscle mass, steady energy levels, calorie deficit]

Please design a high-velocity "Meal Assembly Engine":
- The "Modular Prep" Strategy: Instead of preparing boring, identical pre-made meals in plastic containers, show me how to batch-prep 3 versatile bases, proteins, and sauces that can be mixed and matched in under 5 minutes.
- The 5-Day Fast Menu: Outline a scannable weekly menu featuring breakfasts, lunches, and dinners that leverage this modular approach.
- The Frictionless Grocery Framework: Provide a structured shopping list categorized by grocery department to minimize time spent walking down aisles.
```

## How the modular system works

Instead of making 5 full meals, you make components:

| Category | Examples | Used in |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Brown rice, roasted sweet potato, quinoa | Bowls, sides, wraps |
| Protein | Grilled chicken, hard-boiled eggs, lentils | Any meal |
| Sauce | Tahini, chimichurri, Greek yogurt dressing | Transforms the combination |

Monday: rice + chicken + tahini. Tuesday: sweet potato + chicken + chimichurri. Different meal. Same 20-minute Sunday prep.
