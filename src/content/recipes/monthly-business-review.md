**A monthly review without structure is just a meeting.** This recipe generates a template that turns raw performance data into a scannable document any stakeholder can absorb in under 5 minutes.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a fractional Chief Operating Officer (COO) and business systems analyst. I need to run an internal Monthly Business Review (MBR) for my business/team to evaluate our performance, isolate operational bottlenecks, and align our resources for the upcoming month.

My business model or project focus is: [INSERT DETAILS]

Design a comprehensive, highly scannable MBR document template organized into these distinct zones:
- Executive Summary & Core North Star: A high-level space to state the overarching theme of the past month and our status against our primary North Star metric.
- The Scorecard (The Metrics): A clean, markdown table structure crossing key performance indicators (KPIs) against Target vs. Actual results, complete with a "MoM Change %" column.
- The Retrospective (The Wins & Hard Truths): Bulleted sections analyzing what drove our biggest successes, what unexpected bottlenecks paralyzed our velocity, and what projects we need to kill or pivot.
- Strategic Roadmap: An execution calendar outlining our top 3 initiatives for the next 30 days, including explicit owners, resource dependencies, and success thresholds.
```

## The four MBR zones

| Zone | What it answers |
|---|---|
| Executive Summary | "What was the story of this month in one paragraph?" |
| Scorecard | "Did we hit our numbers?" |
| Retrospective | "Why did we / why didn't we?" |
| Strategic Roadmap | "What are we doing differently next month?" |

## The "Hard Truths" section is the most valuable

Most MBRs document wins and skip blockers. That's how the same bottleneck shows up for three months in a row. A good retrospective names what paralyzed velocity, what assumptions were wrong, and what needs to be killed — not just what went well.
