**The best roasts and the most meaningful compliments both require the same thing: specificity.** Generic insults are lazy and generic praise is hollow. This recipe generates both — tight, personal, and funny — from the same raw material: your actual habits and fixations.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a world-class roast comic who is also a deeply supportive, observant friend. I want you to give me a balanced dose of reality: a sharp, witty, hyper-specific roast immediately followed by a genuinely uplifting, high-conviction compliment.

Please ask me to provide a short, brutally honest self-description, a list of my current hyper-fixations, or a rundown of my daily habits (e.g., my favorite video games, my excessive notes folders, or how I obsessively track my projects).

Once I give you my traits, process them into:
- The Roast: A clever, laugh-out-loud burn that lovingly mocks my over-engineered habits, quirks, or creative fixations. Avoid generic insults—make it punchy and hyper-specific to my data.
- The Antidote (The Compliment): A sincere, grounded validation highlighting how those exact same traits demonstrate high intelligence, execution velocity, or deep artistic dedication.

Tell me what details to drop to initiate the cycle.
```

## The two outputs from the same input

| Output | What it does |
|---|---|
| The Roast | Names the absurdity in your habits — lovingly, specifically, with comic precision |
| The Antidote | Reframes those exact same traits as signals of something genuinely admirable |

## Why the same traits fuel both

The traits that make you a target for a roast are usually the same traits that make you unusually capable or creative. Obsessively organizing your notes folders is ridiculous *and* a sign of someone who takes their thinking seriously. Tracking everything in a spreadsheet is absurd *and* a sign of someone with high standards for their own systems. The flip is the point.

## What to share for the best output

The more specific the input, the sharper the output. "I'm into productivity" is weak source material. "I have 47 active Obsidian notes organized by a custom PARA system I've redesigned three times this month" is exactly the right level of detail. Include:

- Specific apps, tools, or systems you use obsessively
- Your current fixations or hyperfixation topics
- Your weirdest daily habits or rituals
- The things your friends tease you about

## 🔁 Leftover Remixes

**🌶️ Spicy:** *"Give me a second roast/compliment cycle targeting a completely different set of my traits."*

**🧊 Mild:** *"Skip the roast. Just give me the compliment — I want the reframe without the burn."*
