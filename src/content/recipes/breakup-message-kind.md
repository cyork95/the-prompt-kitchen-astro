**The kindest breakup message is not the softest one — it's the clearest one.** Vague language ("maybe someday," "it's just not the right time") creates false hope that prolongs pain. A message that is warm *and* unambiguous gives the other person the respect of a real answer instead of a prolonged limbo. This recipe produces both formats — scaled to relationship depth.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an incredibly mature, emotionally intelligent, and clear-sighted relationship coach. I have made the difficult decision to end a romantic relationship with [INSERT CONTEXT, e.g., someone I've been dating casually for a month, a short-term relationship]. I want to send a written message that is profoundly kind, respectful, and empathetic, but completely unambiguous—leaving absolutely zero room for false hope, negotiation, or mixed signals.

The core reason for the mismatch is: [INSERT REASON CASUALLY, e.g., differing long-term life trajectories, lack of romantic chemistry despite great platonic vibe]

Please generate 2 distinct message variations based on the duration of the connection:
- Version 1 (The Early-Stage Pivot): Short, gracious, direct. Acknowledges the time spent together, highlights a genuine positive quality they possess, but clearly states that I do not see a romantic match moving forward.
- Version 2 (The Short-Term Relationship Closure): Deeper, highly respectful. Explicitly validates the connection we built, frames the decision around a fundamental misalignment of paths or needs, and sets a gentle, clear boundary regarding space to process.

Ensure both versions completely avoid vague filler phrases (like "Maybe down the line" or "It's not you, it's me") that dilute the clarity of the conclusion.
```

## The two message versions

| Version | Length | Depth | When to use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early-Stage Pivot | Short | Gracious and direct | A few dates, casual connection, early-stage interest |
| Short-Term Closure | Medium | Validating and clear | A genuine connection that developed real depth |

## Why "no false hope" is the kindest constraint

"Maybe in a different season" sounds kinder than "I don't see a romantic future here." It isn't. The first keeps the door open in a way that delays grief and sustains hope that won't be realized. The second is harder to read, and it's the one that actually allows the other person to move forward. Clarity is a form of respect — it treats the person as capable of handling truth.

## Phrases the recipe explicitly avoids

- "It's not you, it's me" — deflects without explaining anything
- "Maybe down the line" — creates false hope
- "I just don't have the bandwidth right now" — implies timing is the issue when it isn't
- "You deserve someone who..." — generic and avoidant
- "I hope we can still be friends" — inappropriate to include in the closing of the message itself

## What the Closure version includes that the Pivot doesn't

The Short-Term Closure specifically validates the *relationship* — the genuine connection that existed — while being clear that it ends here. This distinction matters: the early-stage pivot can be briefer because less depth existed. The closure version needs to honor what was real before stating what won't continue.
