A content calendar that just fills slots is busywork. This recipe builds one that moves a cold audience down a real funnel — from first impression to trust to conversion — with a different strategic goal each week.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a master content strategist and growth marketer. I need a 30-day content calendar that doesn't just fill up empty slots, but systematically moves a cold audience down a marketing funnel (Awareness → Engagement → Trust → Conversion).

My niche/business is: [INSERT NICHE]
My target platform is: [INSERT PLATFORM, e.g., LinkedIn, Twitter, TikTok]

Generate a matrix of 30 distinct content ideas grouped into 4 weekly strategic themes:
- Week 1: High-Reach/Awareness (Relatable contrarian takes, broad industry myths, or curated resources to drive shares/saves).
- Week 2: Authority/Educational (Step-by-step breakdowns, case studies, or "how-to" guides proving I know my stuff).
- Week 3: Community/Engagement (Interactive questions, personal origin stories, or vulnerable lessons learned).
- Week 4: High-Intent/Conversion (Addressing friction points, showing behind-the-scenes transformations, or a direct, high-value call to action).

For each idea, provide a working hook/headline and the core message/takeaway.
```

## The four-week funnel

| Week | Type | Audience state | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Awareness | Doesn't know you | Make them stop the scroll and follow |
| 2 | Authority | Curious, evaluating | Make them trust your expertise |
| 3 | Engagement | Warming up | Make them feel seen and connected |
| 4 | Conversion | Ready to act | Make them click, buy, or reach out |

Running these in sequence moves a new follower from "who is this?" to "I trust this person" in roughly a month — then you repeat the cycle.

## Platform-specific notes

Tell the model which platform you're on — the content format changes significantly:

- **LinkedIn:** Written posts, carousels, first-person stories, professional angles
- **TikTok/Reels:** Hook-driven video concepts, trends, high-energy formats
- **Twitter/X:** Short punchy threads, hot takes, conversational questions
- **Instagram:** Visually-led concepts, save-worthy tips, aesthetic cohesion
