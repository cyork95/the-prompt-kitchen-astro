The PAS framework (Problem → Agitation → Solution) is the backbone of almost every high-converting sales page. This recipe builds the whole structure in one pass — each section has a specific psychological job.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a senior conversion rate optimization (CRO) copywriter. Write a high-converting sales page framework for [Product/Service], which targets [Specific Audience Persona].

Structure the output with clear headings for the following sections:

1. Above-the-Fold: A compelling H1 (hook the main desire/pain point) and a clear subheadline explaining the mechanism. Include a placeholder for a primary CTA button.

2. The Agitation: Empathize with 3 specific, frustrating daily problems the target audience faces.

3. The Reveal (The Solution): Introduce [Product/Service] as the antidote. Break down its 3 core benefits (not features) using bold bullet points.

4. Social Proof Blueprint: Describe what kind of testimonial or case study should go here to handle the biggest objection (e.g., price or trust).

5. Risk Reversal & Final CTA: Write a strong guarantee statement and a final, urgent call to action.
```

## What each section is doing psychologically

| Section | Job |
|---|---|
| Above the fold | Capture attention and signal "this is for me" in 3 seconds |
| Agitation | Make the problem feel real and personal — not abstract |
| The reveal | Shift from pain to relief; tie every benefit to an outcome |
| Social proof blueprint | Address the #1 objection before they voice it |
| Risk reversal | Remove the fear of being wrong; make the CTA feel safe |

## Features vs. benefits — the key distinction

The recipe asks for **benefits**, not features. Here's the difference:

- Feature: "Real-time collaboration"
- Benefit: "Your team ships without waiting on each other"

- Feature: "AI-powered suggestions"
- Benefit: "You stop staring at a blank page"

When filling in `[Product/Service]`, describe it in terms of what changes in the customer's life — not what the product technically does.

**💰 Budget:** *"What's the single headline change most likely to improve conversion on this page? Explain the psychology."*
