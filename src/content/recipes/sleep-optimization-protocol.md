**Most sleep advice focuses on what happens at bedtime.** The science says the prep window starts 90 minutes earlier. This recipe builds the full protocol — wind-down, environment, and the 3 AM reset most people handle wrong.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a sleep scientist and neurobiologist specializing in sleep architecture and insomnia resolution. I am struggling with sleep quality (e.g., falling asleep, staying asleep, waking up exhausted) and need an evidence-based system to optimize my sleep efficiency.

My current sleep baseline and struggles: [INSERT SPECIFICS, e.g., screen time before bed, racing thoughts, midnight waking]

Please build a definitive "Sleep Optimization Protocol" encompassing:
- The "Sunset Downshift" Routine: A step-by-step 90-minute evening wind-down sequence that signals melatonin production via light modulation, temperature manipulation, and cognitive brain-dumping.
- Environmental Architecture: A checklist to optimize my physical bedroom environment across three specific variables (ambient temperature, lux/light intrusion, and acoustic dampening).
- The Middle-of-the-Night Reset: Give me a precise, zero-stress protocol to follow if I wake up at 3:00 AM with a racing mind, ensuring I don't trigger cortisol spikes that keep me awake until morning.
```

## The three environmental variables

| Variable | Target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Temperature | 65–68°F (18–20°C) | Core body temp must drop to initiate and maintain sleep |
| Light | Near zero lux | Even low light suppresses melatonin production |
| Acoustics | Below 30 dB, or consistent white/brown noise | Irregular sounds trigger micro-arousals |

## The 3 AM reset — what most people get wrong

The instinct is to check the time, check your phone, or lie there trying harder to sleep. All three spike cortisol. The correct protocol: stay in bed, keep eyes closed, do a slow body scan starting from your feet — physical attention without emotional engagement. If still awake after 20 minutes, get up briefly in dim light, do something boring, return when sleepy. Never check the clock.
