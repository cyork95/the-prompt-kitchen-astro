**Most proposals lose because they describe services instead of solving problems.** This recipe reframes your pitch around the client's business impact, then uses tiered pricing to shift the decision from a yes/no to a choice between options.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an elite freelance consultant and client acquisition expert. I am bidding on a high-value freelance project and need a proposal that shifts the client's perspective from viewing me as a "costly vendor" to an "indispensable partner."

The project description and client pain points are: [INSERT DETAILS]
My unique strategy and background: [INSERT YOUR EDGE/STACK]

Please generate a structured, conversion-focused proposal template containing:
1. The Problem Reframe: Mirror the client's explicit pain points, but reframe them around the larger business implications (revenue lost, delayed shipping, team burnout) to prove deep domain empathy.
2. The Proposed Solution Arc: Outline a 3-phase execution roadmap. Keep it high-level and outcome-oriented so they don't get bogged down in technical minutiae.
3. The "Options" Pricing Framework: Present 3 distinct scope options (e.g., Core Execution, Accelerated Implementation, Complete Full-Scale Transformation) to pivot their decision from "Should I hire them?" to "Which tier do I want?"
4. Explicit Risk-Reversal: End with a low-friction call-to-action and a professional guarantee or milestone that completely eliminates their buyer hesitation.
```

## The tiered pricing shift

Presenting three options does something single-price proposals can't: it moves the client's mental question from "is this worth it?" to "which version do I want?" The middle tier almost always wins — use that to your advantage when setting the price you actually want.

| Tier | Purpose | Pricing rule |
|---|---|---|
| Core Execution | Makes the middle feel reasonable | Price it lower than you'd accept |
| Accelerated Implementation | The one you actually want them to pick | Price it at your target |
| Full-Scale Transformation | Makes the middle feel like a deal | Price it high enough to anchor |

## The risk-reversal finish

End every proposal with something that eliminates the "what if this doesn't work?" fear — a milestone-based payment structure, a revision guarantee, or a first-phase trial. Removing downside risk closes more proposals than any amount of credibility-building.
