**The best workout plan is the one you actually do.** This recipe designs around your real constraints — available days, equipment, and energy variance — not an idealized schedule you'll abandon in week three.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an elite personal trainer and behavioral fitness coach specializing in sustainable physical transformations. I want to build a highly effective workout routine that builds muscle and improves metabolic health without causing burnout or taking over my entire week.

My current metrics and constraints are:
- Fitness Level & Experience: [INSERT EXPERIENCE]
- Available Equipment: [e.g., full commercial gym, dumbbells at home, pure bodyweight]
- Schedule Flexibility: [e.g., 3 days a week, maximum 45 minutes per session]
- Primary Physical Goal: [e.g., hypertrophy, strength, joint longevity, fat loss]

Please design a comprehensive "Progressive Overload Blueprint":
1. The Weekly Split: Break down exactly what muscle groups or movement patterns (e.g., Push/Pull/Legs, Full Body) will be trained on which days.
2. The Workout Matrix: Provide the exact exercises, sets, reps, and rest intervals for each day. Prioritize high-leverage compound movements.
3. The Auto-Regulation Protocol: Teach me how to scale a workout down on days when my sleep or energy is completely crashed so I don't break my consistency streak.

Keep the plan realistic, intensely focused on recovery, and easy to track in a simple note-taking app.
```

## The auto-regulation protocol (why it matters)

Most plans have no answer for the bad day. You wake up under-slept, skip the workout, feel guilty, skip again — and the plan is over by week two.

Auto-regulation gives you a scaled-down version to run instead of nothing:

| Energy level | What to do |
|---|---|
| Full | Run the full program as written |
| 70% | Drop volume 30% — keep intensity, cut sets |
| 50% | Movement-only session — lighter weight, higher reps, no PRs |
| Depleted | 10-minute walk + mobility work. Counts as the streak. |
