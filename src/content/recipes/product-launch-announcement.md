Most launch announcements read like release notes. This recipe leads with the "Why Now" — the shift in the world that makes this product feel inevitable and timely — before introducing what it does.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a Product Marketing Manager. Write a product launch email/announcement for [Product Name], which is a new [type of product/feature] designed to help [Target Audience] achieve [Primary Goal].

The announcement must cover:

- The "Why Now": Frame the launch around a major shift, trend, or long-standing user request in the industry.
- The Big Reveal: Introduce the product with energy and a clear, high-impact value proposition.
- 3 Core Capabilities: Use a clean list format with emojis or bold subheaders to show exactly how it works and what it solves.
- Exclusive Access/Offer: Detail how they can get it (e.g., Beta access, early bird pricing, or rolling out to accounts today).
- Call to Action: A bright, undeniable call to action to try it or learn more.
```

## The "Why Now" is the hardest part — here's how to nail it

The Why Now isn't about your company. It's about the world the customer lives in:

- A market shift: *"AI has changed what your customers expect from support — but most tools haven't caught up."*
- A trend: *"Async-first teams are now the norm, not the exception."*
- A user pain that's boiled over: *"You've been asking for this for two years. It's here."*
- A regulation or external event: *"With [new compliance requirement] coming into effect..."*

The Why Now makes the product feel like a natural response to something already happening — not a thing you're trying to sell.

## Capabilities vs. features (again)

List what users **do** or **achieve**, not what the product technically is:

| ❌ Feature | ✅ Capability |
|---|---|
| "API integration" | "Connect to your existing stack in minutes" |
| "Dashboard" | "See everything that matters in one view" |
| "AI-powered" | "Draft responses in seconds, not hours" |

## 🔁 Leftover Remixes

**🌶️ Spicy:** *"Rewrite this as a Twitter/X thread — same launch, different format. Hook tweet first, then 4-5 follow-ups."*

**🧊 Mild:** *"Write just the subject line and preview text for this launch email — 3 options, ranked by open rate potential."*

**💰 Budget:** *"What's the single most important thing to communicate in the first sentence of this announcement?"*
