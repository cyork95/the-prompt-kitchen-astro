**A self-review is a sales document.** Your manager needs ammunition to advocate for you. This recipe transforms a bullet-point brain dump into a structured, impact-first evaluation that makes that advocacy easy.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a high-level executive coach and professional copywriter. I need to write my self-evaluation performance review for my role as a [INSERT ROLE/TITLE]. I want to showcase my impact, leadership potential, and technical/operational execution without sounding arrogant or overly modest.

My core achievements, metrics, and projects over the last review cycle:
[INSERT ACHIEVEMENTS/METRICS HERE]

Areas where I know I can improve or grow:
[INSERT GROWTH AREAS HERE]

Synthesize my raw inputs into a highly professional, structured performance review formatted into the following standard corporate evaluation sections:
- Executive Summary: A high-impact opening statement framing my trajectory and overall contribution over the past cycle.
- Core Impact & Quantifiable Wins: Translate my achievements into clear "Action ➔ Output ➔ Business Result/ROI" bullets.
- Leadership, Autonomy, & Collaboration: Highlight how I unblocked team members, managed ambiguity, and took extreme ownership of projects.
- Strategic Development Goals: Frame my areas for improvement as proactive, high-leverage growth opportunities for the upcoming cycle.
```

## The Action → Output → Business Result formula

| Raw input | Formatted bullet |
|---|---|
| "Rebuilt the onboarding flow" | "Redesigned end-to-end onboarding → reduced time-to-activation by 40% → decreased first-week churn by 18%" |
| "Helped the team with blockers" | "Resolved 12 cross-functional blockers across Q3 → unblocked 3 product launches → shipped 2 weeks ahead of roadmap" |

## The growth area reframe

Never write "I need to improve my public speaking." Write: "I am prioritizing executive presence development in Q[X], with a target of presenting at [Y] — directly supporting my goal of [Z]." Forward-facing, specific, leverage-oriented.
