**You are the least able to see your current self clearly — you're too close to it.** A future-self letter captures the texture of the present moment: the anxieties, the ambitions, the values you're holding onto, and the questions you haven't answered yet. Reading it years from now is the only way to measure real growth.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an insightful narrative therapist, philosopher, and time-capsule curator. I want to write a deeply intentional, authentic letter to my future self, exactly [INSERT TIME HORIZON, e.g., 1 year, 5 years] from today. I want to capture my current reality, raw ambitions, hidden anxieties, and current worldview so I can measure my genuine personal growth when I open it.

Please interview me one question at a time to extract the emotional and situational layers of my present life. Do not dump the questions all at once. Over our dialogue, explore:
1. The Present Snapshot: What does your daily life look like right now (your current environment, daily rituals, relationships, and major projects)?
2. The Current Crucible: What is the single biggest uncertainty, fear, or friction point you are actively wrestling with in this exact season of life?
3. The Core Unforgivables: What compromises are you terrified your future self might make? What values must your future self hold onto no matter what?
4. The Open Questions: What are 3 profound questions you want to ask your future self to see how your perspective shifts over time?

Once we finish this dialogue, synthesize my answers into a beautiful, deeply moving, and structured "Time Capsule Letter" formatted and ready for me to lock away.

Let's begin. Ask me the first question.
```

## The four interview layers

| Layer | What it captures |
|---|---|
| Present Snapshot | The factual texture of your life right now — environment, routines, relationships, active work |
| Current Crucible | The single hardest thing you're carrying — the uncertainty or fear you haven't resolved |
| Core Unforgivables | The values you don't want to negotiate away — what you refuse to let future-you compromise on |
| Open Questions | The things you genuinely don't know yet — that future-you will be able to answer |

## Why one question at a time

A list of four prompts produces four separate answers. A conversation produces something deeper — your answer to the Crucible often reveals something about the Unforgivables you wouldn't have said directly. The dialogue structure is the extraction mechanism.

## The time horizon matters

A 1-year letter is tactical — it captures specific decisions and worries that will be resolved or transformed by then. A 5-year letter is more philosophical — it captures who you are, what you believe, and what you want, layers that shift more slowly. Both are worth writing; they capture different things.

## What to do with the letter

The letter only works if you actually put it somewhere you won't open it early. A sealed physical envelope. A future-dated email (use a service that delivers email at a future date). A locked folder with a calendar reminder set. The friction of not being able to open it is part of the value — you're preserving a snapshot, not journaling.
