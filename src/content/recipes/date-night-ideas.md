**"Dinner and a movie" is fine. This is better.** Three completely distinct blueprints calibrated to your actual situation — not a generic list of date ideas that assumes you live in a major city with a flexible budget and unlimited energy.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a world-class bespoke experiences designer, romance concierge, and local culture expert. I want to plan an unforgettable, non-cliché date night that completely bypasses the standard, boring "dinner and a movie" routine.

Our current parameters and constraints are:
- The Location & Vibe: [e.g., Cozy at-home night, exploring a local downtown vibe, nature-focused outdoor adventure]
- Budget Tier: [e.g., Free/Low-cost, moderate, premium/splurge]
- Shared Passions/Interests: [e.g., Crafting homemade meals, playing tabletop RPGs, tasting artisanal teas, listening to vinyl records]
- Energy Level: [e.g., Low-demand winding down after a long week vs. High-energy exploratory adventure]

Please design 3 completely distinct, curated date-night blueprints. For each concept, include:
1. The Hook (The core theme or unique activity of the night).
2. The Sensory Menu (A suggestion for a unique flavor, pairing, or musical playlist to set the atmosphere).
3. The Connection Catalyst (One thoughtful, low-pressure question or playful game designed to spark deep, meaningful conversation).
```

## The three blueprint layers

| Layer | What it does |
|---|---|
| The Hook | The central concept — the thing that makes this night distinct from every other night |
| The Sensory Menu | Sound, taste, and atmosphere — the details that make a good plan feel immersive |
| The Connection Catalyst | One question or game that creates actual conversation, not just background noise |

## Why the Connection Catalyst matters most

The Hook gets you there. The Sensory Menu sets the mood. But the Connection Catalyst is what makes the night memorable — a question that neither of you has thought to ask, or a low-stakes game that creates a genuine moment. These are deliberately "low-pressure" so they don't feel like homework.

## The energy level input

This is the input most people skip and then regret. A premium outdoor adventure blueprint is useless on a Friday night after a brutal work week. Matching the plan to actual energy level — not aspirational energy level — is the difference between a date that happens and a date you cancel.
