**History isn't inevitable — it's contingent.** One altered event ripples differently through technology, culture, language, and power across centuries. This recipe traces those ripples across three temporal scales and hands you a visual prompt to see the alternate present.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a master speculative fiction author, cultural anthropologist, and political historian. I want to run an intense simulation exploring a major "What If" historical divergence point to see how a single altered event ripples across centuries.

The historical point of divergence I want to explore is: [INSERT HISTORICAL EVENT & THE PIVOT, e.g., What if the Library of Alexandria never burned? What if the steam engine was successfully commercialized in Ancient Rome?]

Please build an alternate world timeline detailing:
- The Immediate Ripple Effect (The 10-year downstream consequences on technology, borders, and local culture).
- The Century Shift (How global superpowers, language, and societal norms have mutated 100 years later).
- The Alternative Present Day: Describe what daily life, technology architecture, and urban landscapes look like right now in this timeline. Include a copy-pasteable Midjourney/Flux prompt to visually capture a core scene from this alternate reality.
```

## The three temporal scales

| Scale | What it explores |
|---|---|
| Immediate Ripple (10 years) | Direct, local consequences — technology, political borders, cultural shifts closest to the divergence point |
| Century Shift (100 years) | How those changes compound — which powers rise and fall, what languages spread, what social norms emerge |
| Alternative Present Day | The full extrapolation — what today looks and feels like in this timeline, in concrete sensory detail |

## The divergence point framing

The best divergence points have a clear pivot: not just "what if Rome didn't fall" (too vague) but "what if the Roman Empire successfully suppressed the spread of Christianity in the 3rd century AD." Specificity in the premise produces specificity in the ripple. The more concrete your What-If, the more grounded the alternate timeline.

## The Midjourney/Flux prompt

The alternate present-day description is followed by a ready-to-paste image generation prompt — a concrete visual scene from the alternate timeline. This is one of the most satisfying parts: you can actually *see* what the divergence looks like rendered as an image.

## Starting points worth exploring

- What if the Black Death never reached Europe?
- What if the Mongol Empire conquered Western Europe?
- What if gunpowder was never discovered in China?
- What if Carthage won the Punic Wars?
- What if the printing press was invented 300 years earlier?
