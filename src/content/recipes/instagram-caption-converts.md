**Most Instagram captions are afterthoughts.** The visual does the scroll-stopping work, then the caption fails to convert the attention into action. This recipe structures the caption as a direct-response piece — opener, value, explicit ask.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a premium social media manager and direct-response copywriter. I need an Instagram caption that pairs with a visual asset to stop the scroll, deliver high-utility value, and drive an explicit conversion action.

The visual/video context is: [DESCRIBE THE IMAGE OR REEL CONTENT]
The core message I need to deliver is: [INSERT MESSAGE]
My desired business outcome is: [e.g., comment a specific keyword for an automated link, click link in bio, save for later]

Please write a highly formatted, conversion-optimized caption structured as follows:
- The Opener (Line 1): A bold, punchy statement under 125 characters that prevents the caption from being cut off by the "...more" button.
- The Value Stack: Break down the core message using short, single-sentence paragraphs, clean bullet points, and ample negative space to maintain visual rhythm.
- The Micro-Commitment Call to Action (CTA): Explicitly direct the user on exactly what action to take next, telling them what they will immediately receive by doing it.
```

## The 125-character opener rule

Instagram cuts captions after approximately 125 characters in the feed view. If your first line ends there mid-sentence, you lose the reader. The opener should be complete and compelling at the cutoff — the rest of the caption is bonus content for people who tapped "more."

## The micro-commitment CTA

Vague CTAs ("let me know below!") underperform specific ones ("comment GUIDE and I'll send you the full breakdown"). The specificity isn't just clarity — it tells the algorithm that your post drives comment engagement, which expands reach.

| Weak CTA | Strong CTA |
|---|---|
| "Check the link in bio" | "The full checklist is in my bio — it's free" |
| "Let me know what you think" | "Which of these do you struggle with most? Drop it below" |
| "Save this for later" | "Save this — you'll want it when [specific situation] hits" |
