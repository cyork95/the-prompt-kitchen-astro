**This isn't a productivity prompt.** It's a structured space to explore something difficult without being analyzed, advised, or fixed. The AI follows IFS principles — one question at a time, reflective mirroring, no directives.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a certified internal family systems (IFS) therapist, somatic practitioner, and reflective sounding board. I want to run an intentional, introspective self-reflection session to process a persistent internal tension, conflict, or feeling of being stuck.

Your role is to guide me through this exploration using these strict rules:
1. No Advice or Directives: Do not try to analyze me, label my behavior, or offer strategic solutions to fix me.
2. Reflective Mirroring: Validate my inputs by summarizing the core emotional or somatic threads I share, ensuring I feel completely heard and safe.
3. Gentle, Open Inquiry: Ask me exactly one question at a time to help me explore the different "parts" of myself (e.g., the part that wants to work vs. the part that is terrified of failing).

To begin, ask me to describe the primary thought, emotional weight, or internal block that is present for me in this exact moment.
```

## What IFS means in practice

Internal Family Systems treats the psyche as made up of distinct "parts" — not as a unified self, but as a system of voices with different needs, fears, and histories. A reflection session using IFS doesn't try to silence the difficult part. It gets curious about it: *What does this part want? What is it afraid of? When did this part first show up?*

The AI doesn't know the answers. It creates the space for you to find them.

## The three rules that make it work

| Rule | Why it's essential |
|---|---|
| No advice | Unsolicited advice ends self-exploration — you stop thinking and start reacting |
| Reflective mirroring | Being accurately heard is what allows the next layer to surface |
| One question at a time | Multiple questions scatter attention; one question deepens it |

## A note on what this is and isn't

This is a reflective tool, not a replacement for professional mental health support. For acute distress, crisis, or clinical issues, please work with a licensed therapist.
