**Every offer is an opening bid.** Negotiating doesn't risk the offer — not negotiating leaves money on the table that the company budgeted for anyway. This recipe gives you the exact script for the response and the counter-moves if they push back.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an expert executive talent agent and compensation negotiator. I have just received a job offer for the role of [INSERT ROLE] at [INSERT COMPANY TYPE/INDUSTRY], but the initial base salary or total compensation package is below my market value and target.

The initial offer details: [INSERT BASE, EQUITY, BONUS, SIGN-ON, ETC.]
My target compensation and unique leverage points: [INSERT TARGET & EXPERIENCE HIGHLIGHTS]

Please provide a comprehensive negotiation script and playbook containing:
- The Verbal/Written Response: A polished, highly collaborative email or phone script to acknowledge the offer with genuine enthusiasm while clearly stating that the compensation needs adjustment to align with the market and the unique value I bring.
- Total Rewards Levers: Give me a prioritized checklist of secondary variables to negotiate if their base salary budget is strictly capped (e.g., signing bonus, guaranteed performance review windows, remote flexibility stipend, additional equity vests).
- Objection Deflectors: Provide word-for-word scripts to handle common HR deflections like "This is the maximum band for this tier" or "We need to keep internal equity across the team."
```

## Secondary levers when base is capped (ranked by value)

| Lever | Why it works |
|---|---|
| Signing bonus | One-time cost — easier to approve than a permanent base increase |
| Accelerated performance review | 6-month instead of 12-month — earlier chance at raise |
| Equity acceleration or additional grants | Doesn't show up in headcount budget |
| Remote flexibility stipend | Often has its own budget pool |
| Title adjustment | Costs nothing; improves future comp negotiations |

## The two deflectors you'll actually need

**"This is the max for the band"** → *"I understand — is there flexibility on a signing bonus or an early performance review to help close the gap? I'm very excited about this role and want to find a way to make it work."*

**"We need to keep internal equity"** → *"I completely understand the importance of internal equity. Based on my [X years of experience / specific skill / past results], I'd be contributing at a level that I believe warrants [target]. What would it take to get there?"*
