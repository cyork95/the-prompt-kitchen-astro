**The first 72 hours determine whether a subscriber becomes a reader or a name on a list.** This recipe writes all three emails — full word-for-word — with subject lines and preview text included. No templates. Actual copy.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a master email marketer and list retention strategist. I need a high-conversion, 3-part automated "Welcome Email Sequence" to nurture fresh subscribers the moment they join my email list, shifting them from cold signups to deeply engaged fans.

My newsletter niche and target audience: [INSERT NICHE & AUDIENCE]
The core lead magnet or incentive they just downloaded: [INSERT LEAD MAGNET IF APPLICABLE]
My primary long-term goal for subscribers: [e.g., join a premium membership, read weekly essays, buy a course/product]

Please draft the full word-for-word text for three consecutive emails:
- Email 1: Deliver & Delight (Sent immediately). Deliver the value, re-introduce my core mission, and ask a low-friction question to trigger a direct reply (boosting email deliverability rates).
- Email 2: The Shift / Framework (Sent 48 hours later). Share a vulnerable personal story, a massive mistake, or a core framework that flips their conventional thinking about my industry on its head.
- Email 3: The Open Vault / Paid Offer (Sent 72 hours later). Seamlessly introduce my primary offer or best resources, mapping out exactly how it accelerates their transformation.

Include clear, high-CTR subject line options and preview text blocks for each email.
```

## The three-email arc

| Email | When | Job |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Deliver & Delight | Immediately | Deliver the goods, ask a reply question, set the relationship tone |
| 2 — The Shift | 48 hours | Build trust with vulnerability or a framework that challenges their assumptions |
| 3 — The Open Vault | 72 hours | Introduce the offer naturally — not as a pivot, as the next logical step |

## Why Email 1 asks a question

A reply in the first email tells your subscriber's email provider that this is a real relationship — not a newsletter blast. That signal improves deliverability for every email after it. The question doesn't need to be profound: "What's the one thing you're most hoping to get from this newsletter?" works perfectly.

## The preview text field

Preview text is the 40–80 characters visible in the inbox beside the subject line. It's the most underused real estate in email marketing. The recipe generates this separately for each email — keep it in your ESP (email service provider) as a dedicated field, not pasted into the email body.
