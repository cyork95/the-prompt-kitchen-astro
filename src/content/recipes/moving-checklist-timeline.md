**Moving without a system is controlled chaos. Moving with a system is just logistics.** This recipe generates an ironclad, chronological timeline calibrated to your specific household scale and any fragile or complex inventory that needs special handling.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an elite organizational systems designer and relocations coordinator. I am moving to a new residence and need an ironclad, chronological checklist to manage the massive logistics, technical admin, and packing chaos without losing my sanity.

My moving details:
- Current State/Timeline: Moving from [CURRENT CITY/STATE] to [NEW CITY/STATE] in exactly [INSERT NUMBER] weeks.
- Household Scale: [e.g., 3-bedroom house with a backyard/garden beds vs. 1-bedroom minimalist apartment].
- Special Inventory: [e.g., Fragile electronics, complex planted freshwater aquariums, vinyl record collections, delicate plants].

Please map out a high-velocity, milestone-based timeline divided into:
- 4 Weeks Out: Major administrative shifts (utilities transfers, updating address records, secure storage booking, sorting junk).
- 2 Weeks Out: The Room-by-Room Packing Protocol. Give me a strategy to pack efficiently using a labeling/numbering system. Include explicit safety/packing instructions for my stated special inventory items.
- Moving Day War Room: A precise, hourly breakdown of moving day tasks (handling movers, final walkthrough sanity checks, setting up the "First-Night Box" with essentials).
```

## The three milestone phases

| Phase | Core work |
|---|---|
| 4 Weeks Out | Admin — address changes, utility transfers, booking movers, sorting what leaves and what doesn't |
| 2 Weeks Out | Packing — room by room with a labeling system, special handling for fragile or complex items |
| Moving Day | Execution — hourly breakdown, mover coordination, walkthrough, First-Night Box |

## The Special Inventory input is critical

Standard packing instructions don't cover planted aquariums, vinyl record collections, or fragile electronics. Mentioning these explicitly generates specific packing and transport instructions — not generic "use bubble wrap" advice. The more specific your inventory, the more useful the protocol.

## The First-Night Box concept

The single highest-ROI moving prep task. Pack one clearly labeled box or bag that contains everything you need for the first 12–18 hours in the new place: phone charger, toiletries, a change of clothes, coffee setup, snacks, toilet paper, towels. Load it last, unload it first. Every other box can wait.

## The labeling/numbering system

The default failure of rushed packing is boxes labeled "Kitchen — misc." The protocol generates a specific system — usually a numbered inventory with destination room and priority tier (unbox first vs. can wait a week) — so nothing gets lost in the chaos of 40 identical brown boxes.
