Raw meeting notes are a stream of consciousness. This recipe converts chaotic, overlapping thoughts into a structured summary anyone can act on in 30 seconds.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an elite executive assistant. Clean up the messy meeting notes provided below into a highly polished, structured summary.

Organize the output into the following explicit sections:

- Executive Summary: A 2-3 sentence overview of the meeting's main purpose and high-level outcomes.
- Key Decisions Made: A bulleted list of finalized decisions (bold the key terms).
- Action Items Table: Create a Markdown table with columns for: Task, Owner, and Deadline. Extract this explicitly from the notes.
- Next Steps/Open Questions: Any items that require follow-up or were left unresolved.

Here are the messy notes:
[Paste your raw, chaotic notes here]
```

## Tips for better output

**Give it everything.** Paste your raw notes exactly as they are — abbreviations, half-sentences, crossed-out thoughts. The more context, the better the summary.

**Name the attendees.** If your notes mention "Sarah said..." or "the PM wants...", let the AI know who those people are so the Action Items table has real owner names.

**Flag ambiguous decisions.** If something in the notes wasn't clearly decided, mention it. The model will surface it in Open Questions rather than guessing.

## What the output looks like

```
Executive Summary
The Q3 planning meeting aligned on three priorities: feature freeze date, 
headcount request timeline, and the new onboarding redesign scope.

Key Decisions Made
- Feature freeze: **October 15th** (no exceptions without VP approval)
- Headcount request to be submitted by **Sept 30th**

Action Items
| Task | Owner | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Draft headcount request | Sarah | Sept 27 |
| Share wireframes for review | Design team | Oct 3 |

Open Questions
- Budget approval process for contractors still unresolved
```
