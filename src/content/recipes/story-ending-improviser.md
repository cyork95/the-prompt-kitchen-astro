**A weak ending doesn't just disappoint — it retroactively damages everything that came before it.** This recipe generates three structurally distinct endings for any premise, each with its own emotional logic and specific scene composition, so you can feel the difference before committing to one.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an award-winning novelist, master screenwriter, and narrative architect. I am going to give you a rough story premise, a set of character dynamics, or an incomplete plot draft, and I want you to improvise a selection of jaw-dropping endings.

Please ask me to provide my story context. Once I do, generate 3 radically different, high-impact ending options:
- Option 1: The "Twist Knife" Ending (A psychological, entirely earned plot twist that completely recontextualizes the entire story).
- Option 2: The "Poetic Resolution" (An emotionally resonant, bittersweet ending focused deeply on character growth and symbolic closure).
- Option 3: The "Open Loop" Cliffhanger (A high-tension resolution that resolves the immediate conflict but leaves a massive, haunting existential question lingering for the audience).

For each option, outline the exact structural beats and describe the final scene's visual composition. Give me the instructions to paste my premise.
```

## The three ending archetypes

| Ending | Emotional logic | What it requires |
|---|---|---|
| Twist Knife | Recontextualization — the audience re-reads the story mentally | The twist must be *earned*, not imported — it has to be possible given what you've already written |
| Poetic Resolution | Emotional closure — the character arc completes, not the plot | Character transformation must be visible in the final image or action |
| Open Loop Cliffhanger | Intellectual tension — the plot resolves but the theme doesn't | The lingering question must be the *right* question — one the story was always secretly about |

## "Earned" is the operative word for the Twist Knife

A twist that surprises is cheap. A twist that surprises *and* makes the audience immediately reinterpret everything they've already seen — that requires the twist to have been *possible* in the existing material. The recipe asks for this specifically to avoid the cheap imported twist that breaks the story's internal logic.

## The structural beats output

For each ending you get a breakdown of the specific story moves — not just "the character dies" but the scene-by-scene beats that get you there and make the ending land. This is more useful than a summary because it shows you the *shape* of the ending, which helps you evaluate whether it fits your story.

## 🔁 Leftover Remixes

**🌶️ Spicy:** *"After the 3 endings, tell me which one you would choose as the author — and what the story would lose if you chose either of the other two."*

**🧊 Mild:** *"Just give me the Poetic Resolution. My story doesn't need a twist — it needs an honest ending."*
