**Generic itineraries optimize for coverage, not experience.** This recipe builds around your actual pace, interests, and tolerance for chaos — including fallback plans for when weather or energy crashes your original plan.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an elite bespoke travel concierge and logistics architect. I want to plan an incredible, optimized itinerary for an upcoming trip, but I want to avoid generic tourist traps and over-scheduled burnout.

My travel parameters and constraints are:
- Destination: [INSERT CITY/COUNTRY]
- Trip Duration & Dates: [INSERT LENGTH / DATES]
- Traveling Companions & Vibe: [e.g., Solo trip, couple's romantic getaway, family vacation with kids]
- Daily Pace Constraint: [e.g., Slow and leisurely (max 2 main activities/day) vs. High-energy/Fast-paced]
- Absolute Must-Haves/Interests: [e.g., Local hidden food scenes, nature photography, historical walking tours]

Please design a comprehensive "Smart Travel Itinerary":
1. The Daily Rhythm: Break down a day-by-day schedule organized geographically so I am not wasting hours backtracking across a city.
2. Hidden Gems & Audibles: For each day, suggest one non-obvious local spot (café, viewpoint, or shop) and one "Rainy Day/Audible" alternative activity if weather or energy levels crash.
3. Logistics & Booking Checklist: Give me a scannable list of reservations, transit passes, or tickets I must book weeks in advance to ensure a zero-friction trip.
```

## The three itinerary layers

| Layer | What it prevents |
|---|---|
| Daily Rhythm (geographic) | Backtracking and transit waste that kills energy by mid-afternoon |
| Hidden Gems & Audibles | The disappointment of a "must-see" that's crowded, closed, or exhausting |
| Logistics Checklist | The scramble of realizing a popular restaurant or attraction is sold out |

## The pace constraint is the most important input

"Slow and leisurely (max 2 main activities/day)" and "high-energy/fast-paced" produce completely different itineraries. The default failure mode of AI-generated travel plans is over-scheduling — more activities than any person can enjoy without turning a vacation into a sprint. Set this parameter deliberately.

## Geographic organization — why it matters

Most interest-based itineraries cluster by *category* (all museums on one day, all food experiences on another). Geographic organization clusters by *neighborhood* — you see the museum, the coffee shop around the corner, and the viewpoint three blocks away all in the same morning, then move to a different part of the city. Half the transit time, twice the immersion.
