**The quality of your thinking is determined by the quality of your questions.** This recipe generates 9 questions across three cognitive lenses — not to give you answers, but to expose the assumptions, inversions, and scale-shifts you haven't considered yet.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an expert facilitator, Socratic philosopher, and executive coach. I want to deeply explore a specific challenge I am facing, but I don't want you to give me answers. I want you to generate a set of profound, highly targeted questions that disrupt my current mental models and force me to think sharper.

My current challenge, project, or problem state is: [INSERT DETAILS HERE]

Please generate 9 thought-provoking questions divided into three distinct cognitive lenses:
- Lens 1: Questioning Assumptions (Challenging the unexamined "rules" or boundaries I have placed around this problem).
- Lens 2: Inverting the Problem (Using second-order thinking and inversion frameworks to look at the challenge completely backward).
- Lens 3: Scaling Up/Down (Forcing me to consider what this problem looks like if it were multiplied by 100, or if my resources were cut to zero).

Deliver these questions cleanly so I can select one to unpack and reflect on.
```

## The three cognitive lenses

| Lens | What it does | Example question pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Questioning Assumptions | Surfaces constraints you've accepted without examining | "What would be true if the rule you're operating under didn't exist?" |
| Inverting the Problem | Reverses the framing — sometimes backward reveals what forward can't | "How would you guarantee this fails? What does that tell you?" |
| Scaling Up/Down | Tests which elements are essential vs. contingent | "If you had 100x the resources, what would you stop worrying about?" |

## How to use the questions

Pick one. Sit with it. The temptation is to answer all nine — that produces shallow answers to all of them. One question answered deeply is worth more than nine answered quickly.

## Why no answers

The moment the AI gives you an answer, you stop thinking and start evaluating its answer. The questions-only format keeps you in the driver's seat. The insight you generate is more durable than the insight you receive.
