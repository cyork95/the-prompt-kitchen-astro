**Confirmation bias makes most self-made comparisons useless.** You already have a preference — this recipe forces the analysis to find the costs and risks in both options, not just the one you're leaning away from.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an unbiased, data-driven decision theorist and analytical consultant. I am facing a complex choice between two compelling paths and need an objective, multi-dimensional comparison framework that completely bypasses my personal confirmation bias.

The two options I am comparing are:
Option A: [INSERT OPTION A]
Option B: [INSERT OPTION B]

My core values, long-term goals, or situational constraints are: [INSERT CONTEXT]

Please build a comprehensive "Comparative Decision Matrix" structured as follows:
- Strategic Alignment: Evaluate how cleanly each option feeds into my stated long-term goals and constraints.
- The Hidden Friction Audit: Analyze the unstated, downstream costs of each option (e.g., cognitive load, time maintenance, financial debt, relationship strain).
- Asymmetric Risk-Reward Profiles: Map out the worst-case downside vs. best-case upside for both options. Which path has an acceptable failure state and a higher asymmetric upside?
- The Tie-Breaker Directive: Provide an explicit, objective decision rule I can use to make the final call if both options score closely across the matrix.
```

## The Hidden Friction Audit — the section most comparisons skip

Obvious costs are already in your mental model. The hidden ones aren't:

| Hidden cost type | Examples |
|---|---|
| Cognitive load | How much ongoing mental bandwidth does this option require? |
| Time maintenance | How much of your time does it consume on an ongoing basis? |
| Relationship strain | Who does this decision affect, and how do they feel about it? |
| Identity cost | Does this option require you to become someone different? |
| Optionality loss | What doors does this close permanently? |

## The asymmetric framing

The best decision isn't always the one most likely to succeed — it's the one where failure is survivable and success is outsized. A 60% chance of a good outcome and a catastrophic downside may be worse than a 40% chance of a great outcome and a recoverable downside.
