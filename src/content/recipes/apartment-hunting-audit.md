**The property that felt best on a sunny Saturday afternoon isn't always the one you'll be happiest in six months later.** This recipe scores your candidates across five variables — financial efficiency, commute, layout, maintenance signals, and lifestyle fit — so the decision is data-driven, not vibes-driven.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a meticulous real estate strategist, financial analyst, and lifestyle designer. I am currently touring and evaluating multiple property options for my next home, and I need a definitive framework to objectively score them so I don't make an emotional decision I regret later.

Please ask me to paste the parameters, rent/price details, and pros/cons of the top 2-3 properties I am considering. Once I provide them, synthesize the data into an "Urban Living Audit":
1. The Core Scorecard Matrix: Build a markdown table scoring each property on a scale of 1-10 across five crucial variables: Financial Efficiency (rent vs. utility bills vs. square footage), Commute/Walkability, Layout/Natural Light, Asset Maintenance (brittle plumbing, old HVAC signs), and Lifestyle Alignment (space for hobbies, pets, or work-from-home setups).
2. The Hidden Cost Warning: Highlight any unstated downstream costs or friction points unique to each option (e.g., hidden HOA rules, street parking issues, lack of insulation leading to utility spikes).
3. The Tie-Breaker Verdict: Give me a clear, objective decision rule based on my data to help me lock in the winning lease or purchase confidently.

Give me the instructions on how to submit my property options.
```

## The five scorecard variables

| Variable | What it captures |
|---|---|
| Financial Efficiency | Rent vs. utility bills vs. square footage — the real cost per usable foot |
| Commute/Walkability | Time and friction of daily movement — a cheap apartment 45 minutes from everything costs more than you think |
| Layout/Natural Light | How livable the space actually feels day-to-day, not just in listing photos |
| Asset Maintenance | Early signals of deferred maintenance — old HVAC, soft floors, slow drains that become expensive problems |
| Lifestyle Alignment | Whether the space actually fits how you live: WFH setup, pet space, hobby room, hosting capacity |

## The Hidden Cost Warning section

This is where properties that scored well on the matrix can still lose. Common hidden costs:

- **HOA rules** that restrict what you can do, require fees, or create friction with management
- **Street parking** in areas with permit restrictions, seasonal limits, or high competition
- **Old windows and insulation** that make utility bills unpredictable
- **Laundry access** — in-unit vs. shared vs. laundromat is a quality-of-life variable that costs real time
- **Noise** — a great apartment on a loud street or above a busy venue has a real cost on sleep and focus
