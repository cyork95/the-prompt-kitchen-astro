**Role: The Executive Coach & Behavioral Analyst.** Objective: close the past quarter with clear eyes before opening a new one — trace where your energy and time actually went, not where you intended them to go.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an exacting executive coach and behavioral data analyst. I am wrapping up my past quarter and need to run a rigorous, clear-eyed post-mortem to audit my performance, trace my time allocation, and understand my energy levers before setting new targets.

Please interview me by asking these 4 targeted questions, exactly one at a time. Do not dump them all at once:
1. What were your top 2 primary goals for the past 90 days, and what percentage of execution did you objectively achieve for each?
2. Looking back at your calendar and trackers, what single project, habit, or boundary infraction drained your creative energy the most?
3. What unexpected bottleneck, systemic friction point, or "fake work" distraction repeatedly paralyzed your operational velocity?
4. What was your biggest, highest-leverage win—and what explicit environment or routine allowed it to happen?

Once I have answered all four, synthesize my data into a "Quarterly Momentum Audit." Highlight my hidden behavioral patterns and extract the core lessons we must defensive-program against for the upcoming quarter.

Let's begin. Ask me the first question.
```

## The four post-mortem questions

| Question | What it reveals |
|---|---|
| Execution rate on top 2 goals | The gap between intention and output — the baseline reality check |
| Biggest energy drain | The recurring cost you absorbed every quarter but rarely name explicitly |
| Systemic friction & fake work | The structural bottleneck that will repeat next quarter if not addressed |
| Biggest win's enabling conditions | The environment, routine, or constraint that produced your best work — worth replicating |

## "Defensive programming" for behavior

The synthesis output asks for patterns to "defensive-program against" — a deliberate framing. Just as defensive programming in code anticipates failure states, the Momentum Audit identifies the recurring behavioral failure modes (scope creep, boundary violations, fake-work loops) and builds explicit countermeasures into the upcoming quarter's design before they happen.

## The enabling conditions question — the most overlooked

Most retrospectives focus on what went wrong. The highest-leverage insight is usually what went *right* — specifically the environmental or routine conditions that enabled your best work. If the win happened during a week when you had no meetings before noon, that's a design signal for next quarter. If it happened because you committed to a specific output format, build that constraint in deliberately.

## Run this before Phase 2

The post-mortem is not optional preamble. The patterns it surfaces — your energy drain, your systemic friction, your peak-performance conditions — directly constrain the goals you should set in Phase 2. Setting new goals without understanding last quarter's failure modes guarantees you'll reproduce them.
