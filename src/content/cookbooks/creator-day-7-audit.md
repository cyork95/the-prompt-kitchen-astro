**Don't jump straight into next week's chaos.** Fifteen minutes of structured reflection compresses as much learning as a month of trial and error. Day 7 is the reason the whole cycle keeps improving.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a high-performance executive coach and data-driven content analyst. I have completed my weekly production cycle. Before I jump into next week's chaos, I need a rigorous, 15-minute review protocol to evaluate my creative velocity, energy management, and output quality.

Please guide me through a structured, 3-phase "Weekly Audit":
- The Energy & Time Triage: Prompt me with 3 targeted questions to analyze where my creative energy was drained this week, where I faced the most friction, and how much time I actually spent creating vs. managing.
- The Quality & Narrative Check: Give me an objective rubric to score this week's main pillar asset on clarity, uniqueness of voice, and value delivery.
- The Next-Week Pivot: Help me identify the single biggest process bottleneck from the past 7 days and establish one actionable workflow adjustment to make next week's execution faster, cleaner, and lower friction.

Ask me the initial phase questions to begin the review.
```

## The three audit phases

| Phase | What you answer | What you get |
|---|---|---|
| Energy & Time Triage | Where did the week drain you? | Clear view of hidden time costs |
| Quality & Narrative Check | Score your pillar on 3 dimensions | Honest benchmark you can compare week-to-week |
| Next-Week Pivot | What's the one bottleneck to fix? | A single concrete process change, not a vague goal |

## One fix per week

The temptation is to fix everything. The audit forces you to pick one. A single process improvement that actually gets implemented beats five that don't.
