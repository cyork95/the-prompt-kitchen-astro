**Most products lose 40–60% of new users in the first week.** The problem is almost never the product — it's the onboarding. This phase builds the retention system that turns launch-week signups into long-term users and eventual advocates.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a product manager and customer success specialist for early-stage startups. My launch week is complete, and I have a fresh influx of new users and signups. Now, I need to pivot from acquisition to retention, ensuring these users don't churn immediately.

My user metrics from launch week are: [INSERT SIGNUPS/PAID USERS OR CURRENT RETENTION STATE]

Please design a post-launch retention system featuring:
- The First-Time User Experience (FTUE): A framework to optimize my onboarding flow so users hit their "Aha! Moment" (the core value realization) within the first 2 minutes of logging in.
- The Feedback Loop Protocol: An automated mechanism to segment my users into three buckets (Active Users, Power Users, and Churned Users) and distinct email templates to send to each to extract feature requests, bug reports, or exit interviews.
- The Referral Flywheel: A simple, low-code referral or affiliate setup that rewards current users for bringing in their peers, turning my initial launch traction into a sustainable growth engine.
```

## The three user buckets

| Bucket | Definition | Email goal |
|---|---|---|
| Active Users | Logged in 2+ times, used core feature | Feature requests — what do they want next? |
| Power Users | Daily/weekly active, high engagement | Referral ask — they're the ones who'll convert friends |
| Churned Users | Signed up, never returned or cancelled | Exit interview — what went wrong? |

## The Aha Moment framework

The Aha Moment is the specific action inside your product where the user first *feels* the value. Every step between signup and that moment is friction that needs to be removed:

1. **Identify the moment** — what's the one action that makes users say "ok, this is worth it"?
2. **Count the steps to get there** — how many clicks, forms, or decisions does it require?
3. **Eliminate everything before it** — can you get users to that action within 2 minutes?

## Referral flywheel options (by complexity)

- **Simplest:** Manual referral code via ConvertKit or Gumroad — one afternoon to set up
- **Mid:** ReferralHero or GrowSurf — plug-and-play referral tracking
- **Full:** Rewardful or FirstPromoter — commission-based affiliate programs for paid products
