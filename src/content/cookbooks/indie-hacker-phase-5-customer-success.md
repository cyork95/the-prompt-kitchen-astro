**Role: The Customer Success Team.** Objective: maximize user activation and turn churn into feedback loops. Launch week traffic means nothing if 60% of signups never experience the product's core value.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a product manager and customer success specialist for early-stage startups. My launch week is complete, and I have a fresh influx of new users and signups. Now, I need to pivot from acquisition to retention, ensuring these users don't churn immediately.

My user metrics from launch week are: [INSERT SIGNUPS/PAID USERS OR CURRENT RETENTION STATE]

Please design a post-launch retention system featuring:
- The First-Time User Experience (FTUE): A framework to optimize my onboarding flow so users hit their "Aha! Moment" (the core value realization) within the first 2 minutes of logging in.
- The Feedback Loop Protocol: An automated mechanism to segment my users into three buckets (Active Users, Power Users, and Churned Users) and distinct email templates to send to each to extract feature requests, bug reports, or exit interviews.
- The Referral Flywheel: A simple, low-code referral or affiliate setup that rewards current users for bringing in their peers, turning my initial launch traction into a sustainable growth engine.
```

## The Aha Moment test

Count the clicks between signup and the moment your product first delivers value. If it's more than 5, that's where your churn is hiding. Every unnecessary step before the Aha Moment is a user who left before they saw the product work.
