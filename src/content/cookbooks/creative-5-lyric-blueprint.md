**Role: The Evocative Lyrical Poet, Songwriter & Creative Coach.** Objective: push past superficial rhymes and extract deep, raw imagery — with a structural scaffold that channels creative energy instead of leaving you staring at a blank page.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an evocative, deeply lyrical poet, songwriter, and creative coach. I want to write an original piece of verse or lyric, but I need a highly stylized structural framework to push past superficial rhymes and extract deep, raw imagery.

My desired emotional theme or tension: [INSERT THEME, e.g., the bittersweet feeling of a planned transition, the quiet of a garden at twilight]
My stylistic/genre influence: [e.g., dark alternative rock like Type O Negative, minimalist modern poetry, rhythmic folk narrative]

Please provide a customized "Lyric Construction Blueprint":
1. The Image Vault: List 5 hyper-specific, non-cliché sensory nouns and textures (e.g., iron shavings, damp loam, a locked dial) matching my theme to anchor the verse.
2. The Structural Constraint: Give me a specific rhythmic rule or stanza layout to write under (e.g., alternate short sentences with long imagery, avoid using specific emotional words entirely).
3. The Opening Line Launchpad: Provide 3 distinct, high-tension starting lines to instantly break the blank-page friction and establish a compelling vocal rhythm.
```

## The three blueprint components

| Component | What it provides |
|---|---|
| Image Vault | 5 concrete sensory anchors — specific enough to generate unique imagery, evocative enough to carry emotional weight |
| Structural Constraint | A writing rule tailored to your genre — so you're not choosing between infinite structural options mid-draft |
| Opening Line Launchpad | 3 distinct first lines — to establish rhythm and give you something to react to rather than something to invent |

## The Image Vault — why specificity over universality

Generic emotional themes produce generic imagery. "Longing" produces: empty chairs, autumn leaves, faded photographs — the full catalog of poetic clichés. "Iron shavings on a workbench after the last shift" produces something that has never been written before, carries the same emotional weight, and gives the reader a specific sensory experience that activates memory. The Vault generates nouns and textures that are specific enough to be surprising while still being thematically resonant.

The instruction "hyper-specific, non-cliché sensory nouns" filters both dimensions: specificity (not just "metal" but "iron shavings") and freshness (not "autumn leaves" but "damp loam in a crack").

## The Structural Constraint — why rules produce better verse than freedom

Infinite structural freedom produces the same problem as infinite creative options: paralysis followed by default choices. A constraint like "alternate a line under 6 syllables with a line over 12 syllables" forces rhythmic variation that you wouldn't generate under open conditions. A constraint like "never use the words 'feel', 'heart', or 'love' in any form" forces you to show instead of tell. The constraint isn't a limitation — it's the creative friction that produces distinctive work.

## The Opening Line Launchpad — the reaction method

The three opening lines are not suggestions to copy — they're reaction material. Reading a strong first line activates an immediate response: "yes, but differently" or "no, but the rhythm is right" or "that's the second line, not the first." The reaction breaks blank-page inertia because you're now writing *in response to* something rather than writing *from nothing*. Three distinct options cover different tonal registers so at least one generates a reaction worth pursuing.
