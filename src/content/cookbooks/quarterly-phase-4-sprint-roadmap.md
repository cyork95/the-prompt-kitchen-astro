**Role: The Agile Operations Engineer.** Objective: eliminate the procrastination that comes from a 90-day horizon by breaking it into four tight 3-week sprints, each with a single binary milestone that creates real urgency from week one.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an agile project manager and operations engineer. I have my quarterly OKRs locked in, but 90 days is too long of a horizon to maintain peak urgency. I need to break this quarter down into a 12-week execution schedule, divided into four distinct 3-week hyper-sprints.

My quarterly OKR blueprint is:
[PASTE PHASE 3 OUTPUT HERE]

Please architect a chronological "12-Week Sprint Roadmap":
- Sprint 1 (Weeks 1-3) - Momentum & Foundation: Define the single lowest-friction, high-impact milestone to clear immediately to build dopamine momentum.
- Sprint 2 (Weeks 4-6) - Deep Execution: Map out the core technical or creative heavy lifting phase.
- Sprint 3 (Weeks 7-9) - Optimization & Refinement: Focus on editing, testing, data tracking, or early distribution loops.
- Sprint 4 (Weeks 10-12) - The Push & Close: Shipping the final deliverables, running conversions, and prepping the system for landing.

For each 3-week block, provide a clear, binary "Sprint Milestone" so I can cross it off with absolute certainty.
```

## The four sprint phases

| Sprint | Weeks | Mode | Primary question |
|---|---|---|---|
| Momentum & Foundation | 1–3 | Start | What's the easiest high-impact first move? |
| Deep Execution | 4–6 | Build | What's the hardest, most essential work? |
| Optimization & Refinement | 7–9 | Polish | What needs editing, testing, or data? |
| The Push & Close | 10–12 | Ship | What's left to ship and how do I land it? |

## Why 3-week sprints specifically

90 days as a single unit is too long — urgency collapses after week 3, and recovery from a slow start feels impossible. Two-week sprints are too short for meaningful creative or technical output. Three weeks is the minimum horizon for deep work to compound: week 1 to understand the problem, week 2 to execute, week 3 to complete and evaluate.

## Sprint 1 — the dopamine architecture

The Momentum & Foundation sprint is deliberately designed around the *lowest-friction* high-impact milestone — not the hardest work, but the first win. Momentum is not self-generating; it requires an early success to fuel the subsequent harder work. A Sprint 1 that starts with the most ambitious deliverable usually collapses before Sprint 2.

## The binary Sprint Milestone rule

Each sprint closes with a milestone you can state as a binary: "The first draft exists" — not "I worked on the draft." "The API endpoint returns a valid response" — not "I made progress on the API." Binary milestones produce a real signal: either you're on track or you need to adjust scope. Vague milestones produce a false sense of progress that only fails at the quarter-end review.

## Pasting Phase 3 output

The OKR Blueprint from Phase 3 provides the sprint roadmap's input. The Key Results tell you what needs to exist by day 90; the sprint plan works backwards to define what needs to exist by week 3, 6, and 9 to make that possible.
