**Role: The Career Strategist, Philosophical Sounding Board & Human Alignment Coach.** Objective: move from "I'm not sure what I should be doing" to three concrete, divergent life track options — without collapsing under societal expectations or existential anxiety.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an insightful career strategist, philosophical sounding board, and human alignment coach. I am at a major personal crossroads, feeling uncertain about my long-term life trajectory, career alignment, or creative calling. I need a deep, structured framework to explore my options without getting overwhelmed by societal expectations or existential anxiety.

Please walk me through an interactive, multi-lens identity discovery session:
1. The Unfair Leverage Audit: Ask me 2 targeted questions to uncover the rare skills, weird background combinations, or niche obsessions I possess that feel like effortless play to me but look like grueling work to others.
2. The Ikigai Tension Test: Guide me through analyzing my current ideas against four overlapping lenses: What I am genuinely skilled at, what the world actually values/pays for, what I love exploring, and what systemic problems I feel a deep, burning need to solve.
3. The 3-Alternative Worlds Simulation: Once we gather data, help me outline 3 completely divergent, high-potential life tracks for the next 3-5 years (e.g., Track 1: Doubling down on technical domain mastery; Track 2: The asymmetric indie builder path; Track 3: The wild-card creative pursuit).

Let's begin with Phase 1. Ask me your initial leverage questions to open the exploration.
```

## The three exploration phases

| Phase | Lens | What it surfaces |
|---|---|---|
| Unfair Leverage Audit | Your rare advantage | The skills and combinations that are genuinely unusual — not what you're "good at" generically |
| Ikigai Tension Test | Four overlapping lenses | Where your skills, market value, passion, and mission intersect — and where they conflict |
| 3-Alternative Worlds Simulation | Three divergent tracks | Concrete life paths based on your data, not motivational abstraction |

## The Unfair Leverage Audit — why "feels like play to you, looks like work to others"

The standard career advice "find what you're good at" produces generic answers because most people are good at things they've practiced, not things they're unusually suited for. The Audit specifically targets the overlap of rare skill combination, unusual background, and effortless engagement — because that intersection is where defensible advantage lives. "I'm good at writing" is common. "I can explain complex infrastructure decisions to non-technical executives while also being able to implement them" is rare.

## The Ikigai Tension Test — why all four lenses, not just passion

The classic "follow your passion" framing fails because passion alone doesn't produce a sustainable path. The four-lens test surfaces the tensions explicitly:

| Lens pair | What the tension reveals |
|---|---|
| Love + Not paid for | A hobby, not a career |
| Good at + Don't love | A soul-draining competency trap |
| Paid for + Not good at | Temporary at best, fraudulent at worst |
| Mission + Not scalable | A cause, not a business |

The goal isn't to find a perfect overlap — it's to map where you actually are and make conscious choices about which tensions you're willing to navigate.

## The 3-Alternative Worlds Simulation — why three tracks, not one path

Most life planning converges on a single answer. The Simulation deliberately generates three divergent tracks because the question "which path is right?" is often premature — you don't have enough data to know. Three tracks with different risk/reward profiles make the tradeoffs explicit: the technical mastery track has lower asymmetric upside but higher floor; the indie builder track has higher variance and lower floor; the creative pursuit track has the highest identity alignment and the least predictable economics. The simulation doesn't choose for you — it makes the actual choices visible so you can choose deliberately.
