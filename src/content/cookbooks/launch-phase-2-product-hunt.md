**Product Hunt and Hacker News have completely different cultures.** Copy-pasting the same pitch to both is how you get downvoted on HN and ignored on PH. This phase writes them separately, optimized for each community.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an expert copywriter and launch strategist who has successfully launched multiple Top #1 products on Product Hunt and Hacker News. I am preparing my launch assets and need to optimize my positioning for the indie developer community.

My product name is: [INSERT NAME]
The core problem it solves is: [INSERT PROBLEM]
My unique angle/advantage is: [INSERT ADVANTAGE/STACK]

Please generate the following launch copy and media assets:
- Product Hunt Tagline: 3 options under 60 characters that instantly communicate the value and utility.
- The First Comment (The Maker's Story): Write a vulnerable, authentic, and compelling introductory comment detailing *why* I built this, the personal pain point that sparked it, and a special discount/offer for the Product Hunt community.
- Hacker News "Show HN" Pitch: Draft a text-based pitch optimized for the HN crowd. Ensure it focuses heavily on utility, technical implementation/architecture simplicity, and avoids aggressive, salesy marketing speak.
```

## Platform tone differences

| Platform | What the audience wants | What kills your launch |
|---|---|---|
| Product Hunt | Clean value prop, relatable backstory, community offer | Generic SaaS speak, no maker presence |
| Hacker News | Technical honesty, specific implementation detail, low hype | Marketing language, vague claims, no architecture context |

## The maker story formula

Product Hunt rewards vulnerability over polish. The best first comments follow this structure:

1. **The personal pain** — what problem were *you* actually experiencing?
2. **The failed alternatives** — what did you try first that didn't work?
3. **What you built and how long it took** — specificity builds trust
4. **The offer** — a real discount or perk, not a "check it out"
