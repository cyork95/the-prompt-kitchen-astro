**Role: The Master Organizational Architect & Productivity Systems Engineer.** Objective: externalize everything in your working memory, then triage ruthlessly — so you leave with a prioritized execution map, not a longer list.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a master organizational architect and productivity systems engineer. I am experiencing severe cognitive overload and anxiety because my head is completely full of loose threads, administrative tasks, creative ideas, and professional anxieties. I need to clear my working memory completely and transform this chaos into a prioritized, actionable execution map.

Please ask me to dump my raw, unstructured mental chaos. Do not filter or format it.

Once I provide the dump, process it through a strict triage framework and deliver:
- The Velocity Score Filter: A clean markdown table sorting my tasks based on Impact vs. Effort.
- The "One Domino" Task: Isolate the single most critical action item that makes the rest of the list significantly easier or completely redundant.
- The 5-Minute Clear-Out Pile: A list of tasks taking under five minutes that I should clear immediately to restore rapid mental momentum.
- The Storage Vault: A structured, categorized list of long-term ideas or non-immediate projects formatted cleanly to copy-paste directly into my local PARA folders or personal knowledge base.

Give me the instructions on how to submit my raw brain dump text.
```

## The four triage outputs

| Output | What it produces |
|---|---|
| Velocity Score Filter | A prioritized table — every item scored on Impact × Effort so you can see what's high-leverage vs. high-cost |
| One Domino Task | The critical path item — doing this first makes 3–5 other items easier or irrelevant |
| 5-Minute Clear-Out Pile | Immediate momentum — clearing micro-tasks restores a sense of control that makes the harder work feel less overwhelming |
| Storage Vault | Future-you's reference layer — ideas and non-immediate projects stored cleanly instead of left as mental weight |

## Why "dump raw, don't filter" is the first instruction

Most people start a brain dump by trying to organize as they go: "Is this a task or an idea? Where does it belong?" This defeats the purpose. The value of the dump is full externalization — getting everything out of working memory without judgment. Filtering mid-dump means you're still holding cognitive load while offloading. The prompt explicitly instructs not to filter so the dump is complete before triage begins.

## The Velocity Score Filter — what "Impact vs. Effort" means in practice

| Score | Quadrant | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| High Impact / Low Effort | Do first — highest ROI per unit of energy |
| High Impact / High Effort | Schedule deliberately — worth the investment, but plan it |
| Low Impact / Low Effort | 5-Minute Clear-Out candidates — do them or kill them |
| Low Impact / High Effort | Cut aggressively — these items are where energy goes to die |

## The One Domino — what makes a task qualify

A domino task meets one of two criteria: completing it makes other tasks significantly easier (a kickoff email that unblocks three downstream steps), or completing it makes other tasks redundant (a decision that eliminates the need to prepare for both options). The One Domino is not necessarily the hardest or most urgent task — it's the one with the highest collapse ratio.

## The Storage Vault — PARA compatibility

The Storage Vault output is formatted for direct drop-in to a PARA (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archive) system. Ideas get filed under Resources. Long-term projects with a defined outcome get filed under Projects. Ongoing responsibilities without a finish line get filed under Areas. Completed or paused work gets filed under Archive. The Vault separates "I need to keep this" from "I need to act on this" — which is the core cognitive relief the brain dump produces.
