**Role: The Resume Strategist.** Objective: ensure your resume clears the ATS filter *and* gives a hiring manager a reason to keep reading past the summary.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an expert technical recruiter and resume strategist. I want to optimize my current resume for a specific target job description, ensuring it passes automated Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) while remaining compelling to a human hiring manager.

I am going to paste my target job description and my current raw resume experience.

When I do, please execute the following refinement protocol:
1. Keyword Gap Analysis: Isolate the top 7 high-leverage technical skills, methodologies, or cloud tools mentioned in the job post that are missing or weak in my resume.
2. The Action-Impact Refactor: Take 3 of my existing bullet points and rewrite them using the Google X-Y-Z formula: "Accomplished [X], as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]." Ensure the phrasing matches professional engineering or corporate standards.
3. The Summary Hook: Draft a crisp, 3-sentence professional summary for the top of my resume that aligns my background directly with the core problem this company is hiring to solve.

Ask me to paste the Job Description and my Resume to begin.
```

## What Phase 1 delivers

| Output | What it fixes |
|---|---|
| Keyword Gap Analysis | The invisible filter — keywords the ATS is scanning for that your resume currently lacks |
| X-Y-Z Bullet Refactor | Vague experience statements converted into metrics-anchored evidence of impact |
| Summary Hook | The 3-sentence version of "why you, for this role, right now" |

## The ATS reality

Most enterprise and mid-size company applicant systems score resumes before a human ever sees them. The score is largely based on keyword density matching the job description. The gap analysis step tells you exactly which terms to add — not by keyword-stuffing, but by integrating them naturally into rewritten bullets.

## The X-Y-Z formula

"Accomplished [X], as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]" — credited to Google's recruiting team — converts passive job descriptions into active proof points. "Managed data pipelines" becomes "Reduced pipeline latency by 40% across 3 production systems by migrating batch ETL jobs to a streaming architecture on GCP Pub/Sub."
