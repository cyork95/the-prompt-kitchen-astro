**The first 60 seconds are the algorithm's audition.** High early retention tells YouTube to push the video. This recipe produces a structured intro that locks viewers in before they have time to scroll away.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a YouTube audience retention engineer and script doctor. The first 30 to 60 seconds of my video dictate whether the algorithm drops my video or pushes it to thousands of homepages. I need a bulletproof framework to write an unskippable intro that keeps the retention graph flat.

My video title is: [INSERT TITLE]
The thumbnail concept is: [INSERT THUMBNAIL DESCRIPTION]

Please analyze my concept and draft a word-for-word, 60-second intro script outline that executes the following high-retention phases:
1. The Immediate Payoff (0-15s): Validate the title and thumbnail promise instantly. Do not use an intro logo, theme music, or ask people to subscribe.
2. The Stakes & Empathy (15-30s): Aggravate the viewer's problem or amplify the curiosity. Show them *why* staying until the end is vital.
3. The Narrative Roadmap (30-45s): Establish an open loop or preview a massive payoff coming later in the video without giving away the answer.
4. The Frictionless Transition (45-60s): Dive straight into the first core point or story beat seamlessly.

Include specific cues for visual pattern interrupts, text on screen, and sound effects to maximize engagement.
```

## The four phases at a glance

| Phase | Seconds | Goal | What kills it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Immediate Payoff | 0–15s | Validate the click | Intro logo, "welcome back", self-intro |
| Stakes & Empathy | 15–30s | Make them care *now* | Vague setup with no personal stakes |
| Narrative Roadmap | 30–45s | Open a loop, tease the payoff | Revealing the answer too early |
| Frictionless Transition | 45–60s | Get into the content seamlessly | Awkward pivot or scene change |

## Why pattern interrupts matter

YouTube's retention graph is your report card. Every visual cut, text overlay, or audio shift resets the viewer's attention clock. The prompt asks for these cues explicitly — don't remove them when you use the output.
