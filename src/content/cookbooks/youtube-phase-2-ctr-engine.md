**If your packaging fails, the best video in the world gets zero views.** This phase runs before scripting. Your title and thumbnail are your most important creative decisions — treat them as such.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an expert YouTube packaging consultant and CTR (Click-Through Rate) specialist. I know that if my title and thumbnail fail, the best video in the world won't be watched. I need you to develop a high-converting packaging concept for an upcoming video *before* I write the script.

My video topic/concept is: [INSERT VIDEO IDEA]

Please generate a "CTR Packaging Suite" containing:
- 5 Psychology-Driven Title Formats: Provide options using different psychological triggers (e.g., The Curiosity Gap, Counter-Intuitive Truth, High Stakes, The List/Frame, Relatable Pain Point). Keep them under 50 characters so they don't get cut off on mobile devices.
- 3 Visual Thumbnail Concepts: For each concept, provide a highly detailed Midjourney/Flux prompt. Specify the exact layout, focal point, composition rules (e.g., rule of thirds, clean negative space for text), color contrast strategy (e.g., complementary neon highlights against moody backdrops), and cinematic lighting instructions.
- The Title/Thumbnail Synergy: Explain how the text on the thumbnail and the video title will complement each other rather than repeating the same words.
```

## The five psychological title triggers

| Trigger | What it does | Example pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Curiosity Gap | Opens a loop the viewer must close | "The one thing no one tells you about X" |
| Counter-Intuitive Truth | Challenges an assumption | "Stop doing X (it's actually hurting you)" |
| High Stakes | Raises the cost of not watching | "This mistake is costing you X" |
| List/Frame | Promises structured, scannable value | "7 [things] that [outcome]" |
| Relatable Pain Point | Makes the viewer feel seen immediately | "Why your [X] isn't growing (and how to fix it)" |

## The synergy rule

Thumbnail text and title should never repeat the same words — they should complete each other. If your title is a question, your thumbnail text is the teaser answer. If your title names the outcome, your thumbnail shows the before state.

## Phase 2 → Phase 3 handoff

Take your winning title + thumbnail concept into Phase 3 to write the first 60 seconds. The intro must deliver on exactly what the packaging promises.
