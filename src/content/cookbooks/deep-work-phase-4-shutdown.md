**Role: The Knowledge Management Architect.** Objective: offload your working memory before the context fades, so your next session starts at depth instead of spending 20 minutes reconstructing where you were.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a knowledge management architect and workflow optimizer. My Deep Work session is concluding, and I need to execute a rigorous "Context Anchor" protocol to offload my current working memory into a structured, scannable archive before my focus fades.

Here is the raw, incomplete state of my work, notes, and leftover mental loops:
[PASTE CURRENT RAW STATUS / UNFINISHED THOUGHTS]

Please transform this raw output into a clean, markdown-formatted "Session Hand-off Log" containing:
- The Ground Covered: A concise, bulleted list of exactly what was built, refactored, or solved during this block.
- Open Threads & Mental Loops: Isolate the exact unfinished thoughts, uncompiled logic blocks, or unwritten paragraphs that are currently floating in my head.
- Next Session Launchpad: Write the single most high-leverage, frictionless instruction statement I should read at the absolute start of my next session to instantly re-enter a flow state.
```

## The three log sections

| Section | What it preserves |
|---|---|
| Ground Covered | Proof of progress — a list of completed moves that closes the current loop cleanly |
| Open Threads & Mental Loops | The unfinished thoughts that will haunt your background processing until they're written down |
| Next Session Launchpad | The single re-entry instruction — not a task list, but a precise contextual re-anchor |

## Why the shutdown protocol is the most skipped and most important phase

Most people end a session by closing their laptop and moving on. The cognitive context — the partial solution they were mid-way through, the three connected threads they were holding in working memory — evaporates within 30 minutes. The next session starts cold, spending 15–20 minutes reconstructing what was already understood. The Context Anchor eliminates that tax.

## The "Next Session Launchpad" — one instruction, not a list

A task list for next session requires processing. A single launchpad instruction re-anchors your exact cognitive position:

*"Open `auth_pipeline.py` at line 47. The handoff validation logic is incomplete — you were deciding between throwing a ValueError or returning a Result type. Resume there."*

That instruction takes 10 seconds to read and immediately reconstructs the problem context. A task list of "continue working on auth_pipeline, also check tests, also update docs" requires you to re-derive priority before you can start.

## Integrating with Obsidian or a project tracker

The markdown output is formatted to paste directly into an Obsidian daily note or project file. The three-section structure maps cleanly to:
- **Ground Covered** → the "completed" section of your project tracker
- **Open Threads** → open tasks or inbox items
- **Launchpad** → the top item in tomorrow's note

Running Phase 1 with the Launchpad as input closes the loop: the shutdown output from one session becomes the pre-session input for the next.
