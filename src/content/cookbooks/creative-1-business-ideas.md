**Role: The Venture Capitalist, Lean Startup Broker & Consumer Trend Analyst.** Objective: generate viable business concepts that actually fit your constraints — not generic ideas that require $50K and six months to test.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a venture capitalist, lean startup broker, and consumer trend analyst. I want to generate a matrix of highly viable, non-cliché business concepts that leverage my specific strengths and match strict operational filters.

My operational constraints and filters are:
- My Technical/Domain Stack: [INSERT SKILLS, e.g., Python, SQL, Cloud Architecture, Copywriting]
- Capital Requirements: [e.g., Under $500 bootstrap budget, asset-light, micro-SaaS]
- Time-to-Value Target: [e.g., Solopreneur model, must be capable of launching a validation test within 72 hours]
- Business Model Preference: [e.g., Asynchronous recurring B2B subscriptions, usage-based APIs, niche community info-products]

Please generate 4 distinct, high-potential business concepts. For each concept, provide:
1. The Hook (The name and a 1-sentence value proposition matching the Lean Canvas model).
2. The Monetization Architecture (How it makes money and the target pricing tiers).
3. The Immediate Smoke Test (The leanest, lowest-cost way to validate demand before building).
```

## The four filter inputs

| Filter | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Technical/Domain Stack | Constrains to ideas where your existing skills are the moat — not ideas that require hiring |
| Capital Requirements | Forces asset-light concepts — no "build a factory first" proposals |
| Time-to-Value Target | Filters out ideas that can't be tested within your stated window |
| Business Model Preference | Matches revenue structure to your operational preference — recurring vs. transactional vs. usage-based |

## The three concept outputs — what each one actually produces

**The Hook** follows the Lean Canvas value proposition format: for [target customer], who [has this problem], [product name] is a [category] that [delivers this specific value], unlike [existing alternative]. One sentence. No fluff.

**The Monetization Architecture** specifies the exact mechanism: is it a monthly subscription with annual discount, a per-seat model, usage-based pricing per API call, or a one-time purchase with upsell? Vague "charge for it" answers are rejected. Tier structure and pricing rationale are included.

**The Immediate Smoke Test** is the cheapest possible demand validation before writing a line of code or producing any content. Examples: a landing page with a waitlist button and $5 in targeted ads, a cold DM to 20 people in the target niche offering the thing manually, a tweet announcing the concept and measuring reply sentiment. The test is designed to answer one question: do people want this enough to give you their email address or $1?

## Why "non-cliché" is in the brief

Generic business idea generators produce the same 15 concepts regardless of who asks: SaaS dashboard for X, newsletter about Y, consulting service for Z. The filter inputs force differentiation because concepts are generated at the intersection of your specific skill stack + your capital constraints + your business model preference — a combination that is unique to you. The constraint is the creativity mechanism.
