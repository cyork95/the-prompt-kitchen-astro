**Guessing why a video succeeded or failed is how channels stay stuck.** This phase uses four specific YouTube Studio metrics to produce a precise diagnosis — not a vague "post more consistently" recommendation.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a senior data analyst specializing in YouTube Studio analytics. My video has been live for 7 days. Instead of guessing why it succeeded or failed, I want to use cold data to audit its performance and make precise, structural changes to optimize it or fix its trajectory.

Please interview me to gather my data. Ask me for the following 4 metrics one by one (do not dump them all at once):
1. Impressions and Impressions Click-Through Rate (CTR).
2. Average View Duration (AVD) and Average Percentage Viewed (APV).
3. The Retention Graph Behavior (Are there sharp drops in the first 30s? Spikes? A steady downward slope?).
4. Traffic Sources (Is it driving views via Browse, Suggested, or YouTube Search?).

Once I provide these metrics, diagnose the exact bottleneck of the video. Give me an immediate action plan to execute right now (e.g., swapping the thumbnail, changing the title, or refactoring the editing pacing for the next upload) to course-correct my channel's growth velocity.
```

## What each metric diagnoses

| Metric | Low = problem with… | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Impressions CTR | Title or thumbnail packaging | A/B test a new thumbnail or rewrite the title |
| Average View Duration | Content pacing or intro failure | Shorten the intro; add pattern interrupts |
| Retention Graph drop at 0–30s | Packaging promise not delivered | Recut the intro to validate the click faster |
| Traffic Source: mostly Search | Browse/Suggested not picking it up | Repackage with a more curiosity-gap-driven title |

## Why the interview format matters

The prompt asks the AI to collect metrics one at a time — not all at once. This prevents you from pasting a wall of numbers you half-understand. The back-and-forth forces you to actually look up each stat before continuing.

## The loop

Phase 6 feeds back into Phase 2. The diagnosis tells you what to fix in your next video's packaging — closing the growth loop.

*This is a system, not a one-time checklist. Run Phases 2–6 every video. Re-run Phase 1 when you change your channel direction.*
