**Role: The Systems Engineer & Behavioral Guardrail Designer.** Objective: make your specific distraction impulse harder to complete than continuing to work — using behavioral architecture, not willpower.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a rigorous systems engineer and behavioral guardrail designer. I am experiencing a high-distraction environment today and keep unconsciously breaking my focus to check communication tabs, news feeds, or social media. I need an ironclad behavioral script to run the moment I feel the urge to slip.

My target project right now is: [INSERT TASK]
My primary digital vice/distraction tab today is: [INSERT DISTRACTION]

Please generate a "Cognitive Fireproofing Guide":
- The "If-Then" Implementation Intentions: Provide 3 custom, programmable behavioral triggers (e.g., "If my hand moves toward the keyboard to type X URL, then I will immediately stand up and stretch for 30 seconds instead").
- The Digital Friction Protocol: Recommend 2 low-friction methods to introduce severe physical hurdles between me and my distraction vice right now (without needing to install complex enterprise software blocklists).
- The Internal Monologue Reset: Give me a brief, authoritative internal response statement to read silently when the impulse to switch focus hits, interrupting the automated dopamine loop.
```

## The three fireproofing components

| Component | What it intercepts |
|---|---|
| If-Then Implementation Intentions | The automatic motor pattern — redirecting the behavior before the tab loads |
| Digital Friction Protocol | The frictionless path — raising the cost of the vice from 0 to "actually requires effort" |
| Internal Monologue Reset | The dopamine loop narrative — replacing "I deserve a break" with a factual statement that breaks the automatic script |

## If-Then Implementation Intentions — why they work when willpower doesn't

Implementation intentions are pre-committed behavioral scripts: "If X happens, then I will do Y." The research on them is robust — they increase follow-through on goals by 2–3x compared to intentions alone. The mechanism: they transfer the decision from real-time (when you're already partially committed to the distraction) to pre-decision time (when you're still rational). By the time your hand is moving toward the distracting tab, it's too late for willpower. The If-Then fires automatically.

The prompt generates intentions specific to your stated distraction, not generic ones — because "if I feel like checking Twitter" is less effective than "if my hand moves toward the address bar."

## The Digital Friction Protocol — no blocklist software required

Two high-impact friction methods that require no installation:

**Method 1: Log out of the distraction service.** Not "close the tab" — actually log out. The friction of re-entering credentials adds 30–60 seconds of deliberate decision-making to every access attempt. Most impulse checks evaporate in that window. The impulse wants effortless access; it doesn't want authentication.

**Method 2: Physical device relocation.** Your phone in another room isn't just out of sight — it requires a physical trip to access. Research shows this single change reduces smartphone checks by 30–40%. The friction is the distance.

## The Internal Monologue Reset — interrupting the loop mid-impulse

The dopamine loop has a narrative: "I've been working hard. I deserve a quick check. It'll only take a minute." The Reset doesn't argue with this — it replaces it with a factual statement that breaks the automatic continuation: "This impulse will pass in 90 seconds if I don't act on it. My task is still here. I'm returning to it now." No judgment. No willpower. Just a different script running in the same slot.
