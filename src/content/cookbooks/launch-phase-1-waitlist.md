**Launch to an audience, not to silence.** A waitlist built before the product is done is worth 10x the traffic you'll get on launch day without one. This phase builds the engine.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a growth marketer and lean startup strategist. I am building a new micro-SaaS / digital product and want to launch a pre-order or waitlist landing page to gauge real market demand before finalizing development.

My product idea is: [INSERT IDEA]
My target audience is: [INSERT TARGET AUDIENCE]

Please design a comprehensive landing page framework and pre-launch blueprint, including:
1. The High-Converting Hero Section: Write a punchy, benefit-driven headline, a sub-headline that specifies the exact mechanism of the tool, and a compelling Call to Action (CTA).
2. The "FOMO"/Scarcity Lever: Recommend a sustainable strategy to incentivize early signups (e.g., lifetime discount, early-bird pricing tiers, beta access tokens).
3. The 3-Email Nurture Sequence: Draft three concise, automated emails to send to waitlist signups over 2 weeks to maintain high intent, share behind-the-scenes building progress, and prep them for launch day.

Keep the copy entirely free of corporate jargon and tailored to indie hacker aesthetics (clean, direct, and human).
```

## The three deliverables

| Output | What it gives you |
|---|---|
| Hero Section | Headline + sub-headline + CTA — ready to drop into any landing page builder |
| FOMO Lever | A sustainable early-bird incentive that doesn't cheapen the product |
| 3-Email Sequence | Automated nurture over 2 weeks — keeps intent hot until launch day |

## The FOMO lever options (ranked by sustainability)

1. **Lifetime deal** — highest conversion, highest perceived value, riskiest for margins
2. **Early-bird pricing tier** — clean, easy to communicate, easy to close
3. **Beta access tokens** — works well for tools with a waitlist-gated beta

## Phase 1 → Phase 2 handoff

Once your waitlist is live and collecting signups, start building Phase 2 positioning assets. You'll need them ready before launch day arrives.
