**The Feynman Technique in prompt form.** If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet. This recipe forces that clarity — starting from analogy and layering in the technical terms once the intuition is solid.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a world-class professor specializing in the Feynman Technique and cognitive scaffolding. I am struggling to understand a complex academic concept and need to master it for an upcoming evaluation.

The concept/theory I am studying is: [INSERT CONCEPT, e.g., Quantum Entanglement, Keynesian Multiplier, Mitosis]
My current academic level is: [INSERT LEVEL, e.g., College Sophomore, AP High School]

Please guide me through a 3-step learning acceleration protocol:
1. The 5-Year-Old Explanation: Explain this concept using simple analogies, everyday language, and zero academic jargon to establish the baseline intuition.
2. The Deep Dive Scaffolding: Layer back in the formal technical terms, mechanics, and core variables, showing exactly how they connect to the simple analogy.
3. The Active Recall Pop-Quiz: Generate 3 conceptual, scenario-based questions (not simple definitions) to test my understanding. Wait for my response, then grade my answers and correct my misconceptions.
```

## The three-step learning protocol

| Step | What it does | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| 5-Year-Old Explanation | Strips jargon, builds intuition | Forces the AI to find the core idea |
| Deep Dive Scaffolding | Reintroduces technical language with anchors | Connects abstract terms to something concrete |
| Active Recall Pop-Quiz | Scenario-based questions, not definitions | Tests understanding, not memorization |

## The key instruction: "Wait for my response"

The pop-quiz step tells the AI to ask one question and stop — not dump all three at once. This matters. Spacing the questions forces you to actually retrieve the answer before moving on, which is the whole point of active recall.
