**Role: The OKR Architect.** Objective: convert qualitative priorities into binary, quantifiable success metrics that leave no room for "I think I made progress" — you either hit the number or you didn't.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a master of organizational systems and OKR (Objectives and Key Results) architecture. I have my core priorities for the quarter, and I need to translate them into an ironclad framework with binary, quantifiable success metrics so I can track my progress objectively.

My top priorities for the upcoming quarter are: [INSERT PRIORITIES FROM PHASE 2]

Please build a comprehensive "90-Day OKR Blueprint" containing:
1. Objective Framing: Frame each priority into an inspiring, qualitative, action-oriented Objective statement.
2. The Binary Key Results: For each Objective, establish 3 highly specific, quantitative Key Results. Avoid vague metrics like "improve writing" and use hard thresholds (e.g., "Write 12,000 words across 4 core technical assets," "Complete 36 deep work blocks of 90 minutes").
3. The Lagging vs. Leading Matrix: Differentiate between my lagging indicators (the final outputs) and my leading indicators (the daily/weekly inputs I fully control).
```

## The three OKR blueprint components

| Component | What it creates |
|---|---|
| Objective Framing | An inspiring qualitative direction — "Build a technical writing practice that generates inbound" |
| Binary Key Results | Specific quantitative thresholds — "Publish 4 long-form technical posts of 2,000+ words each" |
| Lagging vs. Leading Matrix | Separates what you measure (outputs) from what you control (inputs) |

## Objectives vs. Key Results — the critical distinction

The Objective is a qualitative, motivating direction: *"Establish a portfolio that proves senior engineering capability."* The Key Results are quantitative, binary checkpoints that tell you whether you reached it: *"Complete 4 case study writeups. Receive 2 inbound referrals. Land 1 technical interview from portfolio traffic."* The Objective provides direction; the Key Results provide proof.

## Why "binary" Key Results are non-negotiable

"Improve my writing" is not a Key Result — it's a direction. "Publish 12,000 words across 4 technical assets" is a Key Result — it's either true or false on the last day of the quarter. Binary success criteria eliminate the cognitive negotiation of "I kind of did it" and replace it with objective data. That data feeds directly into Phase 1 next quarter.

## The Lagging vs. Leading Matrix

| Type | What it is | Who controls it | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lagging indicator | The output | Partially — depends on external factors | "4 published posts" |
| Leading indicator | The input | Fully — depends only on your behavior | "24 deep work writing blocks completed" |

Leading indicators are the only metrics you can act on *today*. A lagging indicator that's behind schedule can only be addressed by examining the leading indicators that feed it. Tracking both lets you course-correct mid-quarter instead of discovering the shortfall on day 89.
