**Your video is filmed. Now the algorithm needs to know who to show it to.** Metadata isn't busywork — it's the indexing signal that determines whether YouTube serves your video to the right seed audience or the wrong one.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a YouTube SEO expert and optimization specialist. My video is filmed and edited. Now, I need a comprehensive, search-optimized, and browse-friendly metadata packet to ensure the YouTube algorithm indexes it accurately and pushes it to the right seed audience.

The core topic and script summary of the video is: [INSERT SUMMARY/TRANSCRIPT]
My targeted keywords/phrases are: [INSERT KEYWORDS]

Please generate the following metadata assets:
- Optimized YouTube Description: Write a 3-paragraph, keyword-rich description. The first two sentences must be optimized for search snippets. Include a structured "Timestamps" section placeholder, and a clean call-to-action block for my links/resources.
- Chapter Breakdowns: Suggest logical timestamps and compelling, click-worthy chapter titles based on my summary.
- Core Tags & Categorization: Provide a curated list of 15 highly relevant tags (mix of broad, specific, and long-tail phrases) and recommend the absolute best YouTube category for this upload.
```

## What the metadata packet includes

| Asset | What it does for the algorithm |
|---|---|
| Optimized Description | Search snippet indexing in the first 2 sentences |
| Chapters | Tells YouTube the structure; improves Suggested placement |
| Tags (15 total) | Broad + specific + long-tail covers all traffic source types |
| Category recommendation | Signals YouTube's internal content taxonomy |

## The tag mix that works

- **Broad (3–4 tags):** High-volume, competitive — e.g., "content creation"
- **Specific (7–8 tags):** Mid-tail match to your exact topic — e.g., "YouTube thumbnail design"
- **Long-tail (3–4 tags):** Low competition, high intent — e.g., "how to make YouTube thumbnails in Canva 2025"
