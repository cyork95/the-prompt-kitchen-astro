**Role: The Award-Winning Speculative Fiction Novelist, World-Builder & Cultural Anthropologist.** Objective: build a logically consistent fictional setting that avoids generic genre tropes — one where every cultural detail is downstream of a specific resource or power structure.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an award-winning speculative fiction novelist, world-builder, and cultural anthropologist. I need to build a deeply immersive, logically consistent fictional setting that avoids generic genre tropes and feels like a living, breathing ecosystem.

My genre and high-level premise: [INSERT PREMISE, e.g., a low-fantasy medieval setting centered around rare botanical magic]
The overarching tonal vibe: [e.g., gritty, atmospheric, cozy and nostalgic, dystopian]

Please guide me through a 3-part Socratic world-building protocol. Ask me exactly one question at a time. Over our dialogue, construct:
1. The Core Infrastructure & Scarcity: What resource, energy type, or magic system dictates this world's economy, and what unique societal problem does its scarcity create?
2. Downstream Cultural Mutations: How does this infrastructure shape daily rituals, architecture, slang, and unwritten social taboos for ordinary citizens?
3. The Systemic Flashpoint: What recent micro-event, political pivot, or technical disruption is currently threatening to shatter the world's status quo?

Present the initial foundational question to open the world-building pipeline.
```

## The three world-building layers

| Layer | Question | What it produces |
|---|---|---|
| Core Infrastructure & Scarcity | What resource dictates the economy? | The foundational logic — every other detail derives from this |
| Downstream Cultural Mutations | How does scarcity shape daily life? | The texture — the rituals, slang, and taboos that make the world feel inhabited |
| Systemic Flashpoint | What is currently threatening the status quo? | The narrative engine — why your story is happening *now* rather than 100 years earlier |

## Why infrastructure and scarcity come first

Most world-building starts with aesthetics: the visual look, the geography, the magic system's mechanics. This produces beautiful settings that feel like painted backdrops rather than living worlds. Starting with resource scarcity produces the opposite: a world where every aesthetic detail is the downstream consequence of an economic reality. If botanical magic requires rare root systems that only grow at specific elevations, then: mountain communities have disproportionate political power, lowland cities have black markets, and ordinary citizens have superstitions about soil fertility. The texture emerges logically from the constraint.

## The Socratic one-question-at-a-time protocol — why not a form

A world-building form asks you to fill in predetermined fields. A Socratic dialogue generates fields you wouldn't have thought to fill. The one-question constraint forces a response before the next question is revealed — which means each answer shapes what's asked next. The dialogue produces a world tailored to your specific premise rather than a generic world-building template.

## The Systemic Flashpoint — why your story starts *now*

Every compelling story answers the implicit question: why is this happening now, and not in any other era of this world's history? The Flashpoint identifies the specific micro-event, political pivot, or disruption that has destabilized the existing order enough for your story's events to be possible. Without a flashpoint, your world is a history book. With one, it's a story.
