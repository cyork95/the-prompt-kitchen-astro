**Every production week has a wall. Day 3 is how you get over it.** This isn't motivational advice — it's a structured set of techniques that force words back onto the page.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a writing mentor and creative performance coach. I am in the middle of executing my content piece for the week, but I've hit a creative wall. I'm experiencing writer's block, overthinking my delivery, and staring at a blank screen.

The piece I am stuck on is: [INSERT SCRIPT/ARTICLE DRAFT OR CONCEPT]
The exact spot I am stuck at is: [INSERT SPECIFIC BOTTLENECK, e.g., the introduction, transition, or finding a good analogy]

Please deploy 3 rapid-fire breakthrough techniques tailored to my situation:
1. The "Voice-to-Text" Freewrite: Give me a conversational prompt to answer verbally so I can strip away rigid, overly formal prose.
2. The "Extreme Constraint" Exercise: Force me to write the next paragraph using a strict constraint (e.g., under 50 words, using zero industry jargon, or explaining it to a 10-year-old).
3. The Alternative Frame: Show me how to approach this specific section from a completely fresh perspective (e.g., starting with a failure instead of a lesson).

Provide these exercises immediately so I can break the friction and get words back on the page.
```

## Why three techniques instead of one

Different blocks have different causes. "Voice-to-Text" breaks formality paralysis. "Extreme Constraint" breaks perfectionism. "Alternative Frame" breaks angle blindness. Run whichever one matches what's actually stuck.

## 🔁 Leftover Remixes

**🌶️ Spicy:** *Add "The piece I'm stuck on is [draft]. Now delete the opening line and start with the second-most important idea instead."*

**🧊 Mild:** *Skip the full prompt. Just ask: "I'm writing about [X] and I'm stuck on the intro. Give me 5 different ways to open this."*
