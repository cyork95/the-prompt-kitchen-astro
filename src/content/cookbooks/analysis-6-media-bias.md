**Role: The Neutral Hyper-Vigilant Linguistic Analyst, Media Ombudsman & Formal Logician.** Objective: run a precise rhetorical audit on any article — exposing the ideological framing, sourcing asymmetry, and logical manipulation strategies embedded in the text before they shape your beliefs.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a neutral, hyper-vigilant linguistic analyst, media ombudsman, and formal logician. I am going to paste the complete text of an analytical article, breaking news story, or editorial essay. I need you to run a precise, data-driven audit to extract hidden ideological slants, political biases, and rhetorical manipulation strategies.

Please ask me to paste the text asset. Once I do, execute a rigorous "Media Integrity Audit" tracking the following metrics:
1. Adjective & Emotional Payload Analysis: Isolate the exact loaded verbs, hyperbolic phrases, or emotional modifiers used to unconsciously anchor the reader's sentiment toward a specific political or structural conclusion.
2. The Omission & Sourcing Triage: Evaluate who is cited as an authoritative data point versus who is summarized using vague collective pronouns ("Critics argue," "Many fear"). Note any glaring omissions of counter-evidence, alternative structural metrics, or opposing expert testimony.
3. Framed Dichotomies: Map out where the text sets up false choices, strawman fallacies, or narrative archetypes (e.g., pure victims vs. cartoonish villains) to simplify a hyper-complex, multi-layered systemic reality.
4. The Neutral Recalibration: Rewrite the single most biased, rhetorically charged paragraph from the text into a completely neutral, objective, and purely factual data report.

Give me the instructions on how to submit my article text.
```

## The four audit components

| Component | What it exposes |
|---|---|
| Adjective & Emotional Payload | The affective manipulation layer — the words that make you feel before you think |
| Omission & Sourcing Triage | The authority asymmetry — who gets a name and title vs. who gets a vague pronoun |
| Framed Dichotomies | The false binary — where a complex issue is compressed into a two-sided contest |
| Neutral Recalibration | The baseline — what the paragraph would say if it contained only verifiable facts |

## Adjective & Emotional Payload Analysis — what "loaded" means specifically

A loaded word carries emotional or ideological valence beyond its denotative meaning. "Officials" is neutral. "Regime officials" is loaded — "regime" implies illegitimacy. "Spending" is neutral. "Reckless spending" is loaded — "reckless" evaluates before the reader has seen the evidence. "Protesters" is neutral. "Agitators" is loaded in one direction; "demonstrators" is loaded in another. The audit identifies these words, maps the direction of the intended sentiment shift, and counts their frequency.

## The Omission & Sourcing Triage — the pronoun test

Named sources with credentials ("Dr. Jane Smith, epidemiologist at Johns Hopkins") carry attributable accountability. Unnamed collective pronouns ("critics argue," "experts say," "many fear") don't. The asymmetry in a piece reveals who the author is centering as credible and who they're dismissing with a vague summary. The triage also asks: what data, expert, or perspective would be required to present a complete picture of this topic — and is it present?

## Framed Dichotomies — the three most common structures

**The false binary:** "Either we act now or we face catastrophe" — a two-option framing that excludes the range of intermediate or alternative responses.

**The strawman opposition:** "Critics of this policy simply don't care about [sympathetic group]" — a characterization of the opposing position that no serious proponent of that position would recognize.

**The archetypal casting:** clear heroes with pure motivations and clear villains with corrupt ones — a narrative structure that maps poorly onto the actual complexity of systemic issues, where actors typically have mixed incentives and partial information.

## The Neutral Recalibration — what the rewrite produces

The recalibration rewrites the most rhetorically charged paragraph using only: verifiable statistics with sources, neutral descriptive language with no emotional modifiers, and active voice that names agents and actions without evaluative framing. The comparison between the original and the recalibration makes the rhetorical layer visible in a way that abstract description cannot. Reading both versions side by side is the most direct available demonstration of how framing shapes perception.
