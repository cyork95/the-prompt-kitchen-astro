**Communities are the highest-ROI distribution channel for indie hackers — and the most dangerous if you do it wrong.** Spam kills your reputation in communities that took years to build. This phase shows how to give value first and mention your product second.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an organic growth hacker and community specialist. I want to seed my product inside niche communities (e.g., subreddits, Discord servers, X/Twitter circles, Indie Hackers groups) without getting banned for blatant self-promotion or spamming.

My product niche is: [INSERT NICHE]
The spaces my target audience hangs out in are: [INSERT COMMUNITIES/PLATFORMS]

Please build an organic distribution playbook containing:
- The "Value-First" Post Template: Create a template for a high-value community post that solves a common community problem using a case study or lesson learned from my building journey, where my product is only mentioned casually as the tooling infrastructure.
- 3 Cold DM/Email Swipes: Write three ultra-short, frictionless outreach templates targeting early adopters or industry micro-influencers. Focus on asking for feedback rather than asking for a sale.
- Community Guidelines Check: Give me a quick, 4-step checklist to evaluate whether a platform is safe to post to, and how to build rapport with community moderators beforehand.
```

## The value-first post structure

A community post that works isn't a product announcement — it's a useful piece of content where your product is a footnote:

1. **Lead with the lesson** — something you learned while building that genuinely helps the community
2. **Show the work** — numbers, screenshots, specific details that prove it's real
3. **Mention the tool** — one casual sentence: "I built [X] to solve this for myself"
4. **End with a question** — invites replies, signals you're there to contribute

## The cold DM rule

Ask for feedback, never a sale. "Would you be willing to try this and tell me what's broken?" converts better than any discount code.

With community posts scheduled and outreach sent, lock in your launch day War Room checklist. Phase 4 is the 24-hour operational guide.
