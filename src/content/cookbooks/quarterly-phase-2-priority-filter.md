**Role: The Systems Strategist.** Objective: convert a chaotic wishlist into a maximum of 3 objectives that don't conflict with each other — and name everything that's being deliberately deferred.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an elite systems strategist and venture consultant. I want to map out my upcoming 90-day horizons. I have a chaotic wishlist of ideas, habits, and professional milestones, but I know that if everything is a priority, nothing is. Help me filter my ambitions down to a maximum of 3 core, high-leverage outcomes.

My raw ideas, professional targets, and personal goals for the upcoming quarter are:
[INSERT RAW WISHLIST HERE]

Please process my raw list through a rigorous strategic filter and deliver a structured "Quarterly Focus Matrix":
- The High-Leverage Objectives: Distill my chaos into 2-3 clearly defined, non-conflicting objectives for the next 90 days.
- The "Domino" Indicator: For each objective, identify the single most critical lever—the one goal that, if achieved, makes all the secondary goals significantly easier or completely unnecessary.
- The Non-Goal Alignment: Explicitly list what high-interest projects or habits I must intentionally put on the back burner or "drop" for the next 90 days to protect my bandwidth.
```

## The three Focus Matrix components

| Component | What it produces |
|---|---|
| High-Leverage Objectives | 2–3 non-conflicting goals that are genuinely achievable in 90 days |
| Domino Indicator | The single lever per objective that unlocks the most downstream value |
| Non-Goal Alignment | The explicit permission to defer — without this list, deferred goals become passive guilt |

## Why "non-conflicting" is the key constraint

Most goal lists fail not because the goals are wrong but because they conflict — they compete for the same time, energy, or cognitive bandwidth. A quarter with one creative output goal and one audience-building goal and one financial optimization goal is three different modes of work fighting for the same focused hours. The filter specifically checks for conflicts and resolves them before you commit.

## The Domino Indicator — the most valuable output

Every goal cluster has one lever that, if moved, makes the others dramatically easier. For a content creator, writing the foundational long-form piece might be the domino — once it exists, the social clips, the newsletter, the course outline all derive from it. For a developer, shipping the core feature might be the domino — everything else (docs, marketing, user feedback) follows. The filter identifies this explicitly so you don't spread effort equally across all objectives.

## The Non-Goal list — why it requires explicit naming

Deferred goals that aren't named become ambient anxiety. You know they exist; you know you're not working on them; you feel guilty. The Non-Goal list converts that ambient state into a deliberate strategic decision: "I am intentionally not pursuing X this quarter because Y and Z are higher leverage." That decision is freeing, not limiting. The goal hasn't disappeared — it's scheduled for consideration next quarter.
