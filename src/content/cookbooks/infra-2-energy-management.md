**Role: The Human Performance Coach & Chronobiology Specialist.** Objective: stop scheduling work by calendar slots and start scheduling by biological reality — aligning your hardest cognitive work with your natural peak windows.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a human performance coach and chronobiology specialist. I am tired of tracking just my time; I want to build a sustainable "Energy Management System" that aligns my hardest creative or technical work with my natural biological peaks, preventing mid-day crashes and burnout.

Please interview me one question at a time to map my biological baseline. Do not dump all the questions at once:
1. The Wake-Up Graph: What time do you naturally wake up, and how long does it take for your brain to feel fully operational without caffeine?
2. The Slump Zone: At what specific points in the day (e.g., 2:00 PM) do you experience a massive drop in focus, clarity, or physical energy?
3. The Second Wind: Do you experience a natural surge of creative or technical focus later in the evening or night?
4. The Current Commitments: What are the rigid, non-negotiable hours of your current day-job or schedule?

Once I have answered all four, synthesize my data into a "Circadian Energy Matrix." Map my day into three zones (Deep Focus, Administrative/Shallow, and Complete Recovery) and show me exactly when to schedule my highest-leverage work to match my biological peaks.

Let's begin. Ask me the first question.
```

## The four baseline inputs

| Question | What it reveals |
|---|---|
| Wake-Up Graph | Your true cognitive ramp time — not when you're awake but when you're actually sharp |
| Slump Zone | Your post-lunch cortisol trough — the time block to protect from high-stakes work |
| Second Wind | Whether you're a chronotype with a late creative surge worth capturing |
| Current Commitments | The fixed constraints the matrix must work around, not through |

## The Circadian Energy Matrix — the three zones

Once the four inputs are collected, the output maps your day into:

**Deep Focus Zone** — your 2–4 hour biological peak window. This is where your highest-leverage work lives: writing, coding, complex analysis, creative output. Guard this window with the same commitment as a standing meeting with your most important client.

**Administrative/Shallow Zone** — your post-peak trough and mid-day transition. Email, scheduling, replying to messages, administrative processing, simple to-dos. Work that needs to happen but doesn't require full cognitive engagement. This is where shallow work belongs — not because shallow work is bad, but because doing it during peak hours is the most expensive mistake in time management.

**Complete Recovery Zone** — deliberate non-work. Not "passive distraction" but genuine recovery: exercise, non-screen time, social connection, sleep. Most high-performers skip this zone. Without it, the Deep Focus Zone erodes within weeks.

## Why chronobiology beats willpower

Willpower is a depletable resource. Chronobiology is a fixed cycle. Scheduling your hardest work during your biological trough and then using willpower to compensate is fighting your own physiology — and losing slowly. The Energy Matrix doesn't ask you to work harder during bad hours. It asks you to stop wasting peak hours on shallow work that could happen at 2 PM anyway.
