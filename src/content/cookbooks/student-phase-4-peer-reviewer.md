**A draft is just a hypothesis.** Phase 4 is where you stress-test it. The four-lens audit doesn't rewrite your voice — it finds the exact lines where your argument weakens, your tone slips, or your logic jumps without a bridge.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a meticulous peer reviewer and academic copyeditor. I have written a draft of a section/essay and I need a rigorous, line-by-line critique to elevate its quality to an "A" grade standard.

Here is my draft text:
[PASTE DRAFT HERE]

Please audit my writing through these 4 academic lenses, providing specific "Before vs. After" refactoring examples:
1. Argumentation & Evidence: Where are my claims unsubstantiated, logically weak, or reliant on assumptions? Where do I need to insert stronger evidence or citations?
2. Tone & Academic Register: Identify and eliminate informal phrasing, passive voice, weak verbs, or repetitive sentence structures. Elevate the vocabulary to be precise and authoritative.
3. Flow & Transitions: Do my arguments transition smoothly between paragraphs, or does the logic feel disjointed and abrupt? Show me how to bridge the gaps.
4. Redundancy & Clarity: Clip out any academic fluff, padding, or wordiness that dilutes the impact of my core points.
```

## The four lenses

| Lens | What it catches |
|---|---|
| Argumentation & Evidence | Claims made without proof; assumptions dressed as facts |
| Tone & Academic Register | Informal phrasing; weak verbs ("things," "stuff," "very") |
| Flow & Transitions | Paragraph breaks that feel like topic jumps instead of logical steps |
| Redundancy & Clarity | Padding sentences that add length but not meaning |

## "Before vs. After" is the key instruction

The prompt asks for specific refactoring examples — not just "this sentence is weak." That forces the AI to show you what the fix looks like, not just where the problem is. Keep that instruction in the prompt.
