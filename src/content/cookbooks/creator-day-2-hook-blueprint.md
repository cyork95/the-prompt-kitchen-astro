**Attention is won in the first 15 seconds and held through deliberate pattern interrupts.** This recipe produces a full script structure with exact beats — not just an outline.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an elite scriptwriter and audience retention specialist. I have chosen my core concept for the week, and I need a high-retention script/outline that maximizes watch time and engagement.

The chosen concept/angle is: [INSERT CHOSEN ANGLE FROM DAY 1]
My primary platform is: [INSERT PLATFORM, e.g., YouTube, Short-form Video, Long-form Article]

Please write a highly structured script blueprint containing the following precise beats:
- The 15-Second Hook: Validate the viewer's click immediately, open a compelling psychological loop, and banish all generic intros ("Hey guys, welcome back").
- The Narrative Stakes: Explicitly state why solving this problem or hearing this perspective matters *right now*.
- The Core Content Delivery: Format the body into clean, punchy sections. Include explicit "Pattern Interrupt" cues (e.g., change of pace, B-roll callouts, visual graphics) every 30-45 seconds to combat drop-off.
- The Seamless Transition: Instead of a formal sign-off that tanks metrics, bridge the final takeaway directly into an active, high-value call-to-action or next step.

Provide the full script text or granular outline based on these rules.
```

## The four beats explained

| Beat | What it does | Common mistake |
|---|---|---|
| 15-Second Hook | Validates the click, opens a loop | Generic "welcome back" intro |
| Narrative Stakes | Makes the viewer care *now* | Burying the "why" in the middle |
| Pattern Interrupts | Resets attention every 30–45s | Letting one long section run unbroken |
| Seamless Transition | Converts instead of just ending | "Thanks for watching, see you next week" |
