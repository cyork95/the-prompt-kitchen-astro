**Role: The Master Space Planner, Professional Organizer & Home Logistics Coordinator.** Objective: transform a high-friction zone into a functional, visually calm space with a sustainable maintenance system — not just a one-time tidy that reverts within a week.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a master space planner, professional organizer, and home logistics coordinator. I want to completely declutter and systematically reorganize a high-friction area of my home to maximize its physical utility, eliminate visual chaos, and build a sustainable maintenance routine.

The target zone/room I want to optimize: [INSERT ROOM/ZONE, e.g., a home office, an attached greenhouse/workspace, a storage closet]
The primary friction points and clutter profiles: [e.g., loose charging cables, scattered physical notes, unorganized hobby supplies]
My organizational style preference: [e.g., ultra-minimalist, functional and accessible, concealed storage]

Please design a comprehensive "Spatial Architecture Plan" structured as follows:
- The 4-Bin Sort Protocol: Provide a strict, binary sorting rule to process every item in the space within a single afternoon, completely bypassing sentimental stagnation or decision fatigue.
- Zoning & Ergonomic Layout: Map the physical space into 3 distinct operational zones based on usage frequency (e.g., Prime Zone for daily tools, Secondary Zone for weekly assets).
- The Cable & Micro-Asset Management Strategy: Provide specific, clean methods to bundle, label, and store small components, tools, or electronics out of sight.
- The 5-Minute Maintenance Audit: Give me a rapid, frictionless evening routine to reset the space to its baseline state without turning it into a grueling chore.
```

## The four plan components

| Component | What it solves |
|---|---|
| 4-Bin Sort Protocol | Decision fatigue — a binary rule processes every item without deliberation |
| Zoning & Ergonomic Layout | Access friction — daily tools should require zero steps to reach; weekly tools, a few steps |
| Cable & Micro-Asset Management | Visual noise — loose cables and small components are the primary source of perceived chaos |
| 5-Minute Maintenance Audit | Entropy — a nightly reset prevents accumulation from rebuilding the original problem |

## The 4-Bin Sort Protocol — the binary rule

The four bins are: **Keep & Place** (this item has a home in this space), **Relocate** (this item belongs somewhere else in the house), **Donate/Sell** (this item has value but not to me), **Discard** (this item has no value to anyone). The binary rule: every item gets assigned to a bin in under 10 seconds. If you're deliberating longer than 10 seconds, it goes in Donate. The protocol bypasses sentimental stagnation by making the decision rule external — you're not evaluating sentiment, you're answering a binary question: does this item have a defined home in this space?

## Zoning & Ergonomic Layout — the three zones

**Prime Zone** (arm's reach from primary work position): tools used daily. Keyboard, mouse, primary notebook, dominant hand tools, active cables. Zero steps required.

**Secondary Zone** (1–3 steps from primary position): tools used weekly. Reference materials, secondary equipment, project supplies for active projects. A brief retrieval is acceptable.

**Archive Zone** (requires deliberate movement to access): tools used monthly or less. Backup supplies, seasonal items, completed project archives. Out of sight entirely.

## The 5-Minute Maintenance Audit — what the evening reset covers

Five moves, one minute each: (1) return all Prime Zone items to their defined positions, (2) file or discard all loose paper, (3) coil and store all cables not actively in use, (4) clear any non-zone items back to their correct zone or room, (5) wipe the primary surface. The audit works because it's a checklist of physical moves, not a judgment about whether the space is "clean enough." The standard is binary: items are in their defined positions or they aren't.
