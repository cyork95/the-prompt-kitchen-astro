**Good content gets ignored when it looks rough or reads like a wall of text.** Day 4 is where the writing becomes readable and the visuals become shareable.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a senior copyeditor and multimedia designer. My core content asset is drafted, and I need to polish the writing for maximum readability while locking in my final production assets (thumbnails, promotional graphics, or video overlays).

Here is my current draft:
[INSERT DRAFT HERE]

Please refine this asset through two parallel tracks:
1. Copyediting for Rhythm: Polish the text to improve its visual and auditory pacing. Break up dense walls of text, vary sentence lengths for impact, bold high-leverage phrases judiciously, and ensure it sounds entirely human and distinct.
2. AI Image Production: Based on the final tone of this draft, generate 2 hyper-detailed Midjourney/Flux prompts. One should be for a high-resolution, photorealistic cover image (specifying camera angles, lighting conditions like golden hour or moody studio, and crisp depth of field), and the other for a minimalist, abstract graphic to support the text.

Deliver the edited text alongside the copy-pasteable AI image prompts.
```

## What "Copyediting for Rhythm" actually produces

- Walls of text → short punchy paragraphs with breathing room
- Uniform sentence length → varied rhythm that reads faster
- Generic phrasing → bolded high-leverage lines that draw the eye
- AI-sounding prose → language that sounds like *you*

## Day 4 → Day 5 handoff

Once your polished asset and image prompts are ready, take the completed piece into Day 5 to atomize it across every channel.
