**Role: The Ad Agency Creative Director & Master Growth Marketer.** Objective: build a unified campaign concept with a single narrative spine, a channel-specific funnel, and a shareability mechanism — so the campaign compounds instead of just running.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a brilliant ad agency Creative Director and master growth marketer. I need a high-impact, multi-channel marketing campaign concept to launch an asset, build a brand presence, or convert prospects into users.

The product/offer I am marketing: [INSERT OFFER DETAILS]
My core conversion metric target: [e.g., drive newsletter signups, book consultation retainers]
My available execution runway/channels: [e.g., organic text threads on X/LinkedIn, side-project marketing tools, targeted newsletters]

Please engineer a comprehensive "Campaign Activation Blueprint":
- The Big Creative Hook: A single, unifying narrative theme or contrarian angle that stops the scroll and instantly sets the brand apart from industry dogma.
- The 3-Part Content Ingestion Funnel: Map out the execution steps across my selected channels, showing exactly how a cold viewer travels from initial attention to high-trust conversion.
- The Low-Friction Viral Mechanism: Recommend a simple, high-shareability element (e.g., a downloadable template, a programmatic scoring matrix, a public challenge) that naturally incentivizes the target audience to share the campaign with their peers.
```

## The three campaign blueprint components

| Component | Function |
|---|---|
| Big Creative Hook | The narrative spine — every piece of content connects back to this single angle |
| 3-Part Ingestion Funnel | The conversion architecture — maps the cold-to-warm-to-converted user journey |
| Low-Friction Viral Mechanism | The amplification layer — the element that makes sharing more natural than not sharing |

## The Big Creative Hook — contrarian angle vs. category claim

Most campaign hooks make a category claim: "The best X for Y." This is forgettable because every competitor makes the same claim. A contrarian angle takes a position the category hasn't taken: "Everyone teaches you to write prompts. We teach you to think in prompts." A contrarian hook works because it creates a clear us/them frame — viewers either agree and feel understood, or disagree and engage anyway. Either response is reach.

The hook is not a tagline — it's a narrative premise that can generate 20 pieces of content. "We teach you to think in prompts" generates: an essay about why writing prompts is the wrong frame, a thread comparing prompt-writing vs. prompt-thinking, a YouTube video titled "Stop Writing Prompts," and so on. The hook is a campaign, not a sentence.

## The 3-Part Ingestion Funnel — what the stages actually are

| Stage | Medium | Job |
|---|---|---|
| Attention | Scroll-stopping hook post on primary channel | Makes a cold viewer stop and read the first sentence |
| Trust | Long-form content, thread, or case study | Demonstrates that you understand their problem better than they do |
| Conversion | Specific CTA to the conversion metric | Offers them the thing they've decided they want after stages 1 and 2 |

The funnel is channel-specific because the same content doesn't work across all channels. An X thread that builds trust is different from a LinkedIn post that builds trust — not because the message changes but because the format, length, and reader expectation differ. The blueprint maps each stage to your stated channel.

## The Low-Friction Viral Mechanism — what "low friction" means

A referral program is not low-friction — it requires signing up, generating a link, and incentivizing sharing with a reward. A downloadable template that says "share this with anyone building in public" on the last page is low-friction — sharing happens as a natural extension of using the thing. The mechanism should: (1) be genuinely useful on its own, (2) be easy to forward or share in its native format, and (3) carry the brand naturally rather than requiring a promotional ask. Templates, calculators, scored assessments, and public challenges all meet these criteria when designed for the specific audience.
