**Role: The World-Class Academic Research Synthesizer, Cognitive Engineer & Structural Knowledge Architect.** Objective: extract the foundational frameworks and actionable mental models from a dense non-fiction book — and surface the author's blind spots — formatted directly for an Obsidian knowledge base.

## The Recipe

```
Act as a world-class academic research synthesizer, cognitive engineer, and structural knowledge architect. I want to deeply parse a dense, high-utility non-fiction book to extract its foundational frameworks, hidden case studies, and systemic mental models while throwing away any repetitive corporate filler or narrative padding.

The book title and author I want to extract: [INSERT BOOK DETAILS]
My exact domain/focus area right now: [INSERT CONTEXT]

Please process this title into a pristine, highly scannable "Knowledge Repository Document" formatted natively for an Obsidian personal knowledge base using clean markdown syntax:
- The Core Thesis Box: A single, 3-sentence block summarizing the author's primary contrarian argument or framework shift.
- The 5 Functional Mental Models: Isolate and deconstruct the top 5 structural models, algorithms, or behavioral rules established in the text. Translate them into actionable code-like blueprints (Input → Mechanism → Output).
- The Operational Playbook: A bulleted, chronological execution roadmap showing how a modern professional can deploy the book's core insights directly into their daily routines or technical pipelines.
- The Critique & Missing Vector: Identify the primary logical gaps, historical inaccuracies, or extreme edge cases that the author completely ignored or over-simplified in their work.
```

## The four repository components

| Component | What it replaces |
|---|---|
| Core Thesis Box | Chapter-by-chapter summaries — three sentences capturing the central argument is more useful than a plot summary |
| 5 Functional Mental Models | Passive reading notes — Input→Mechanism→Output format makes models immediately executable |
| Operational Playbook | General takeaway lists — a chronological deployment roadmap with specific daily anchors |
| Critique & Missing Vector | Uncritical consumption — identifying gaps protects against over-applying a framework to contexts the author didn't test |

## The Core Thesis Box — three sentences, not a synopsis

Most book summaries describe what happens in each chapter. The Core Thesis Box captures only the author's central argument — the thing they're claiming is true and that most people currently get wrong. Three sentences forces compression: what does the author claim, what evidence is the claim based on, and what does the author say you should do differently as a result? Everything else in the book is elaboration of these three sentences.

## The 5 Functional Mental Models — the Input→Mechanism→Output format

Raw mental models extracted from books are often described as principles: "Focus on the process, not the outcome." This is memorable but not executable. The Input→Mechanism→Output format translates the principle into a procedure:

**Input:** You have an outcome goal (ship the product by Friday)  
**Mechanism:** Identify the single next physical action that advances the project, ignoring the outcome entirely  
**Output:** Daily forward motion without outcome-anxiety paralysis

The format makes the mental model something you can actually run, not just agree with.

## The Critique & Missing Vector — why it's the most valuable component

Every influential non-fiction book has a thesis that was tested in specific contexts and then generalized beyond those contexts. The Critique identifies where the generalization breaks: the historical period the data was drawn from, the demographic the case studies represent, the edge cases the framework doesn't cover. Reading without this component produces over-application — using a framework in domains where it was never validated. The Missing Vector makes the model's boundaries explicit so you apply it only where it actually works.
