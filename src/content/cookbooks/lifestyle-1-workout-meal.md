**Role: The Elite Personal Trainer, Performance Nutritionist & Metabolic Health Specialist.** Objective: synchronize your training and nutrition into a single integrated system — so your food is timed to your workouts and your workouts are scheduled around your life, not against it.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an elite personal trainer, performance nutritionist, and metabolic health specialist. I want to build a highly integrated, synchronized fitness and nutrition blueprint that optimizes my physical recovery, protects my time, and fits perfectly into a busy week.

My metrics, equipment, and dietary parameters are:
- Experience Level & Available Equipment: [INSERT TRAINING BASELINE & GEAR]
- Time Budget: [e.g., 3 days a week, maximum 45 minutes per session]
- Dietary Focus & Macro Targets: [e.g., High-protein, macro-conscious, whole-foods focus]
- Primary Physical Goal: [e.g., Lean muscle hypertrophy, joint longevity, sustained energy]

Please build an integrated "Metabolic Sync Engine":
1. The Unified Weekly Matrix: A scannable calendar combining my training days with my daily nutritional focus, showing exactly how to align carb or protein timing with my workout windows.
2. The Targeted Workout Blocks: Provide the exact exercises, sets, reps, and rest intervals, focusing entirely on high-leverage compound movements.
3. The "Modular Assembly" Meal Prep: Give me a lean system to batch-prep 3 versatile bases, proteins, and clean sauces that can be cross-combined into macro-precise meals in under 5 minutes.
```

## The three engine components

| Component | What it replaces |
|---|---|
| Unified Weekly Matrix | Separate workout and meal plans that ignore each other — training on days when your nutrition is depleted |
| Targeted Workout Blocks | Generic 5-day splits — a 3-day compound-focused schedule builds more total volume per session and fits real schedules |
| Modular Assembly Meal Prep | Daily cooking decisions — 3 bases × 3 proteins × 3 sauces = 27 distinct meals from one Sunday prep session |

## The Unified Weekly Matrix — why training and nutrition must be planned together

Carbohydrate timing is the primary lever for workout performance. Training on a low-carb day when the plan didn't account for it produces worse output and slower recovery than the same session on a carb-timed day. The Matrix makes this explicit: training days get pre-workout carb windows and higher total carbohydrate allocation; recovery days shift toward higher protein and fat with reduced total calories. The plan shows both dimensions simultaneously so you're not managing two separate documents that contradict each other.

## The Targeted Workout Blocks — compound movements over isolation

High-leverage compound movements (squat, hinge, press, pull, carry) produce more total muscle stimulus per unit of time than isolation work because they recruit multiple muscle groups simultaneously. A 45-minute session built around 3–4 compounds with progressive overload produces better results than a 90-minute session diluted across 8–10 isolation exercises. The blocks specify exact set/rep/rest parameters rather than leaving program design decisions to the middle of the workout.

## The Modular Assembly Meal Prep — the 5-minute meal architecture

**Three bases:** a grain (rice, quinoa, or oats), a legume or starchy vegetable, and a leafy green or cruciferous vegetable.  
**Three proteins:** a batch-cooked animal protein, a plant-based protein option, and a quick-assembly protein (eggs, canned fish, cottage cheese).  
**Three sauces:** a high-protein base (Greek yogurt or tahini), an acid-forward dressing (lemon-herb), and a warm sauce (tomato or miso-ginger).

Any combination of one base + one protein + one sauce produces a macro-trackable meal in assembly time only. The variety comes from the combinations, not from cooking anything new each day.
