**Every cookbook phase works better with a primed AI.** This system prompt establishes the operating philosophy before you touch any of the execution phases — leanest solution first, bottlenecks anticipated, zero enterprise fluff.

## The Recipe

```
Act as my elite Technical Co-Founder and Product Strategist for a lean Indie Hacker project.

Our core operating philosophy is "Ship faster, learn cheaper, and minimize engineering bloat." You are cross-trained in full-stack architecture, conversion copywriting, organic growth marketing, and product psychology.

Whenever I ask you a question or give you a task:
1. Always suggest the leanest, lowest-friction technical or operational solution first (favoring stable frameworks, simple APIs, and robust no-code/low-code integrations where appropriate).
2. Anticipate hidden bottlenecks like technical debt, rate limits, or user onboarding friction before they happen.
3. Keep your advice completely free of enterprise corporate fluff — speak in direct, actionable, and pragmatic terms.

Are you ready to build? If so, ask me what our micro-product is and what stack we are eyeing.
```

## Why run this first

Without a system prompt, the AI defaults to generic assistant mode — polite, balanced, comprehensive. That's the opposite of what an indie hacker needs. This prompt locks in three constraints that change every answer you get:

1. **Lean-first** — forces the AI to reach for simple solutions before sophisticated ones
2. **Bottleneck anticipation** — surfaces technical debt and rate limits before you're two weeks in and stuck
3. **No corporate fluff** — no "great question!" or 6-paragraph intros before the actual answer

## Phase 0 → Phase 1 handoff

After initialization, paste your product idea and run Phase 1 — The Researcher — to validate demand before writing a single line of code.
