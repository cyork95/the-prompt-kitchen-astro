**Passive re-reading is the worst study method.** This recipe transforms your static notes into a live exam simulation — one question at a time, scored, with specific feedback on exactly what you missed.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an exacting but patient Socratic examiner. I want to prepare for an upcoming exam by turning my static study notes/readings into a dynamic, active retrieval practice session.

Here are the notes/textbook chapters I am being tested on:
[PASTE STUDY NOTES/TEXT HERE]

Please run the following exam-prep protocol:
1. Highlight the 3 most heavily weighted concepts or high-probability exam topics hidden within my text.
2. Ask me exactly ONE challenging, open-ended question at a time based on those concepts. Do not give me multiple-choice options.
3. Once I reply, evaluate my answer critically. Rate my accuracy on a scale of 1-10, pinpoint the exact gaps or nuances I missed, and provide the correct framing before moving to the next question.

Let's begin. Ask me the first question.
```

## Why one question at a time

Dumping five questions at once is closer to a worksheet than a study session. The single-question format keeps you in retrieval mode — you have to produce the answer, get feedback, adjust, then produce again. That spacing is what makes it stick.

## What "high-probability exam topics" means

The AI is asked to identify the 3 concepts most likely to appear on an exam before quizzing you. This is a useful forcing function — if those aren't the concepts your professor emphasized, use that as a signal to adjust your notes before running the quiz.

## 🔁 Leftover Remixes

**🌶️ Spicy:** *After each question, add: "If I score below 7, give me a follow-up question on the same concept before moving on."*

**🧊 Mild:** *Remove the scoring and just ask for "what I got right, what I missed, and the key correction."*
