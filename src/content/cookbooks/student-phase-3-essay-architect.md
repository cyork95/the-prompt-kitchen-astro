**The blank page problem is an architecture problem.** Most essays fail not in the writing, but in the planning. This recipe builds the structure first — a precise thesis, a section-by-section roadmap, and the counter-arguments addressed before the grader can raise them.

## The Recipe

```
Act as an elite academic writing coach and developmental editor. I have to write a major essay/paper, and I want to construct a bulletproof thesis and structural outline before I start drafting.

My essay prompt/topic is: [INSERT ASSIGNMENT PROMPT]
The required length/format is: [e.g., 2,000 words, APA format]

Please collaborate with me to build an "Essay Architecture Matrix":
- The Thesis Sharpener: Help me transform a weak, descriptive thesis into an argumentative, nuanced, and highly debatable thesis statement.
- The Structural Roadmap: Break down my target word count into a logical paragraph-by-paragraph or section-by-section outline. For each section, define its core objective, the specific claim it must prove, and the type of evidence required.
- Counter-Argument Triage: Identify the most likely counter-arguments or objections a critical grader would raise against my thesis, and show me how to proactively address them in my outline.
```

## Descriptive vs. argumentative thesis

| Weak (descriptive) | Strong (argumentative) |
|---|---|
| "Climate change affects coastal cities." | "Rising sea levels will disproportionately displace low-income coastal communities within 20 years, making federal relocation policy the most urgent climate adaptation priority." |
| "Social media has changed communication." | "Algorithmic amplification of outrage on social platforms has accelerated political polarization more than any ideological shift in the electorate." |

A strong thesis is debatable — someone could reasonably disagree with it. A descriptive thesis is just a fact.

## Phase 3 → Phase 4 handoff

Once the architecture is complete, write the draft. Then bring it into Phase 4 for the peer review pass.
